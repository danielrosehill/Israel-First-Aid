## Dehydration Protocol (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### Definition
Dehydration is a decrease in body fluid concentration, occurring when the body loses more fluids than it consumes. One of the most common heat-related injuries during summer and sharav (heat wave) periods in Israel.

### Causes
- Insufficient water intake
- Excessive fluid loss through sweating, breathing, digestion
- Lack of rest during physical activity in heat
- Dehydration and heat stroke are distinct conditions -- dehydration results from lack of fluids; heat stroke from heat accumulation without proper cooling

### Symptoms by Severity

**Mild/Early Stage**
- Weakness
- Dizziness
- Nausea
- Loss of motivation and energy

**Progressive (if untreated)**
- Rapid pulse
- Hallucinations
- Loss of consciousness (severe)

### Treatment

**Mild Cases**
- Fluid intake through the digestive system (oral rehydration)
- Oral absorption is more effective than IV administration for mild/moderate cases
- Moderate salt-containing food is recommended if patient refuses hospitalisation

**Severe Cases**
- When patient cannot drink due to vomiting or swallowing difficulty, IV fluids may be necessary
- Blood pressure measurement before IV fluid administration
- Emergency department evaluation warranted (patients lose both fluids AND electrolytes)

## Child-Specific Information (from Paediatric pages)

### Diarrhoea and Vomiting in Children (from Emergency Situations page)
- Intestinal infections cause fluid loss and absorption problems
- Main concern is dehydration leading to hypovolaemic shock
- **Fluids to AVOID**: cola, fruit juices, plain water, tea (do not replace lost electrolytes)
- Use ORS (oral rehydration solution) or electrolyte-containing fluids

### When to Seek Medical Care for Children
- Blood appears in stool
- Fever exceeds 40 degrees C
- Child shows signs of dehydration:
  - Dry mouth
  - No tears when crying
  - Appears lethargic
  - Produces insufficient urine
- Child vomits yellow or green fluid

## Israeli Climate Context
- Sharav (hamsin) heat waves create extreme dehydration risk
- Desert regions (Negev, Arava, Dead Sea area) are particularly dangerous
- School field trips in desert areas are a common context for paediatric dehydration
- Ichud Hatzalah publishes seasonal guidance for hikers and travellers
