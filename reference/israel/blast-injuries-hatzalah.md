## United Hatzalah's Role in Blast / Trauma Response

United Hatzalah operates 7,000+ volunteer medics with specially equipped ambucycles (motorcycle ambulances), achieving average response times of 3 minutes or less. During October 7, 2023, 1,500 United Hatzalah volunteers were engaged on the first day, treating and transporting thousands of injured.

Their training programs cover first aid, bleeding control, triage under challenging circumstances, and tactical trauma kit usage.

## United Hatzalah Trauma Protocol

### Distinguishing Injury Mechanism
United Hatzalah training emphasises distinguishing between blunt and penetrating trauma:
- **Penetrating injuries** (shrapnel, knife, gunshot): Higher spontaneous pulse recovery rates
- **Blunt trauma** (blast displacement, structural collapse): Internal hemorrhage often proves insurmountable

### Priority: Hemorrhage Control BEFORE Resuscitation
**Critical principle**: "Do not initiate resuscitation before controlling severe bleeding."

Tourniquet application is the preferred initial intervention for limb hemorrhage: "Indirect pressure -- tourniquet application -- is preferable because it is powerful, rapid, and more likely to stop bleeding with certainty" compared to bandages and pressure dressings alone.

For neck/throat bleeding where tourniquets cannot be applied, direct pressure points are used instead.

### Resuscitation Sequence (if needed)
- Standard CAB protocol: Circulation, Airway, Breathing
- Airway: Jaw-thrust manoeuvre (NOT head-tilt, to protect potential spinal injury)
- Ideally performed by two responders: one maintains airway, second operates bag-valve-mask

### When NOT to Resuscitate
- Only in cases of obviously unsurvivable injuries (decapitation, pelvic destruction)
- All other scenarios require resuscitation attempts until ALS teams intervene

## Additional Protocols

### Medications in Trauma (EMT/Paramedic level)
- Epinephrine is generally **contraindicated** in hypovolemic trauma resuscitation (blood vessels already maximally constricted from shock)
- Exception: If cardiac cause suspected with trauma as secondary injury

### Fluid Management
- Goal: Maintain minimum systolic pressure of 90 mmHg (100 mmHg for head injuries)
- Larger-gauge IV lines (14-18G) deliver fluids significantly faster
- Fluids increase circulating volume but do not replace blood

### Temperature Management
- Hypothermia worsens shock outcomes
- Actively warm patients and remove wet clothing

### Multi-Casualty Triage
- Resources focus on patients with better survival prospects
- Low-probability resuscitation patients deprioritised when resources are limited
