## United Hatzalah's Role in Fire / Smoke Emergencies

United Hatzalah volunteers respond to fire and smoke inhalation emergencies as part of their general medical emergency mandate. With 7,000+ volunteers and average response times under 3 minutes, they often arrive before fire services.

### Documented Response Example

In a building fire in Shlomi, United Hatzalah EMTs responded to rescue a woman and nine children from a burning building. Responders who arrived by ambulance "rushed a mother and one of the children who had visible signs of smoke inhalation to the hospital."

This illustrates the standard approach:
1. Rescue / remove from danger (coordinated with neighbours and other responders)
2. Identify smoke inhalation symptoms
3. Rapid transport to hospital

## Expected Protocol (Based on Training Framework)

While specific step-by-step protocols are not published online, United Hatzalah's Family Safety Course and EMT training cover the following standard emergency medicine approach:

### Smoke Inhalation
1. **Scene safety** -- do not enter a burning structure; coordinate with Fire and Rescue (102)
2. **Remove to fresh air** -- move the patient away from smoke exposure
3. **Assess breathing** -- monitor for respiratory distress, coughing, wheezing, hoarseness
4. **Position upright** -- sitting position aids breathing
5. **Administer oxygen** (if equipped -- EMT/paramedic level)
6. **CPR if needed** -- begin resuscitation if patient stops breathing
7. **Rapid transport** -- consistent with United Hatzalah's general approach of rapid response and handoff to hospital care

### Tear Gas Exposure
1. **Move upwind** to fresh air
2. **Do not rub eyes** -- flush with water
3. **Remove contaminated clothing**
4. **Monitor for respiratory deterioration**
5. **Call 1221 or 101** if symptoms persist or worsen

## United Hatzalah Dispatch Support

United Hatzalah's dispatch centre can guide callers through first aid steps over the phone. Dispatchers are trained to "pinpoint locations and guide the caller through vital first aid steps" while volunteers are en route.

For smoke inhalation emergencies, callers can expect phone guidance on:
- Whether it is safe to approach
- How to move the patient to fresh air
- How to position the patient
- When to start CPR

## Differences from MDA

| Aspect | MDA | United Hatzalah |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Primary role in fire | Full EMS response with MICUs | First response (ambucycle), then handoff |
| Oxygen delivery | MICU-equipped | Depends on volunteer equipment level |
| Mass evacuation | Deploys buses and 20+ ambulances | Volunteer-based rapid response |
| Published protocols | Limited but some public content | No public-facing protocols found |

Both organisations coordinate with Fire and Rescue (102) and follow Ministry of Health standards.
