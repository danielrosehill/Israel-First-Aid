## General MDA Smoke Inhalation Guidance

### Key Principles
- Smoke inhalation is the inhalation of toxic fumes or combustion by-products
- Can cause serious injury or death through toxic gas exposure, thermal airway damage, and oxygen displacement
- Children are much more sensitive to inhalation injuries and should be assessed even with light exposure

### Initial Signs and Symptoms
- Shortness of breath
- Cough
- Chest pain
- Blue lips (cyanosis)
- Dizziness
- Altered consciousness
- Stridor (noisy breathing -- indicates upper airway swelling)

### First Aid Steps
1. Move person away from dangerous area to fresh air
2. **Call MDA immediately (101)** and Fire Department (102)
3. Seat victim in comfortable upright position to ease breathing
4. Administer high-concentration oxygen if available
5. Monitor breathing continuously
6. Be prepared to perform CPR if breathing stops
7. Do NOT re-enter hazardous area

### Types of Inhalation Hazards (from MDA training context)

**Carbon Monoxide (CO)**
- Colourless, odourless
- From incomplete combustion in enclosed spaces
- Binds to haemoglobin much more strongly than oxygen
- Pink/reddish lips may be a sign

**Cyanide**
- Released when plastics, foam, paints burn
- Prevents cells from producing energy
- Can be absorbed through inhalation

**Tear Gas (relevant to Israeli context)**
- Causes severe irritation of eyes, nose, mouth, and lungs
- Primarily affects mucous membranes
- In enclosed spaces, concentration can become dangerous

## Child-Specific Considerations

### Why Children Are More Vulnerable
- Smaller airways (smaller diameter means proportionally greater obstruction from swelling)
- Higher respiratory rate = inhale more toxins per kilogram of body weight
- Closer to ground level where heavier gases concentrate
- Less lung reserve
- Faster respiratory compromise
- May not be able to self-evacuate

### Paediatric Assessment
- Children should be checked even with light smoke exposure
- Stridor in children indicates significant airway swelling
- Croup-like symptoms after smoke exposure = urgent
- Monitor for delayed onset of respiratory distress (can develop hours later)

### Israeli Context
- Lag B'Omer bonfires are a common cause of smoke inhalation in children
- Apartment fires in winter (space heater use)
- Tear gas exposure during security incidents near residential areas
- MDA responds to fire scenes alongside Fire Department (102)
