## Definition of Poisoning

Hebrew original: "הרעלה היא ההשפעה המזיקה של חומר כל שהוא לרעה על הגוף"

Translation: Poisoning is the harmful effect of any substance on the body. It can occur through:
- **Ingestion** (swallowing)
- **Inhalation** (breathing in)
- **Skin contact** (including mucous membranes)
- **Injection**

Nearly any substance introduced into the body can be toxic depending on the amount and time period of exposure.

## Signs and Symptoms

Severity depends on:
- The specific toxin involved
- Amount ingested or exposed to
- Speed of absorption
- Individual metabolism rate
- Patient age (critical factor)

### Minor Symptoms
- Mouth irritation
- Blurred vision
- Nausea
- Gastrointestinal discomfort

### Severe Symptoms
- Confusion
- Drowsiness
- Heart palpitations
- Arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat)
- Breathing difficulty
- Restlessness

## Treatment Approaches

United Hatzalah's protocol describes three levels of treatment:

### 1. Decontamination
- **Never induce vomiting** -- causes additional chemical burns and aspiration risk
- Activated charcoal (within 1--2 hours of exposure) can bind toxins in the stomach -- but this is a medical-level intervention, not first aid
- Stomach lavage (gastric lavage via tube) is possible only in medical settings
- Bowel irrigation in extreme cases (hospital only)

### 2. Antidotes (Medical-Level)
Available for specific poisons -- administered by medical professionals only:
- **Naloxone** for opioid overdose
- **Flumazenil** for benzodiazepine overdose

### 3. Supportive Care -- "This Is Our Primary Role as First Responders"

Hebrew original: "זה השלב הרלוונטי עבורנו"

The first responder's main role is supportive care while the body naturally processes and eliminates the toxin:
- **Maintain airway patency** -- keep the airway open and clear
- **Monitor vital signs** -- breathing, pulse, level of consciousness
- **Support breathing** -- be prepared to perform CPR if needed
- **Fluid support** -- only as directed by medical control
- **Blood pressure support** -- positioning, warmth

---

## Critical Actions for First Responders

1. **Collect all substance containers and packaging** -- bring them to the hospital or have them available for the arriving medical team. This helps identify the poison and determine the quantity ingested.
2. **Identify the substance** through history-taking and physical examination
3. **Contact the Poison Information Center at Rambam Hospital, Haifa** for guidance on treating specific poisonings

---

## Key Differences from MDA Protocol

Both organisations agree on the fundamental principles. Notable points from United Hatzalah's presentation:

- United Hatzalah explicitly frames the first responder role as **supportive care** ("this is the stage relevant to us"), distinguishing clearly between what a first responder does versus hospital-level interventions
- The emphasis on **decontamination** (activated charcoal, gastric lavage) is presented as context/education rather than a first-aid action -- these are hospital procedures
- Both sources agree: **do NOT induce vomiting**
- Both sources agree: **preserve the substance/container**
- Both sources direct to the **Israel Poison Information Center at Rambam** for specific guidance

## Gaps and Limitations

- The United Hatzalah page does not provide the Rambam Poison Information Center phone number directly (04-7771900) -- it references the centre but omits the number
- The page does not include specific guidance for different routes of exposure (inhalation vs. skin contact vs. ingestion) in separate sections
- No specific mention of recovery position for unconscious patients
- No Israel-specific seasonal or climate context
- The page is oriented towards EMT/volunteer training rather than lay public first aid
