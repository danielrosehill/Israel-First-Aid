## General MDA Hypothermia Protocol (All Ages)

MDA training materials cover hypothermia as part of climate injury modules. Key points from available MDA sources:

### Definition
- Hypothermia occurs when core body temperature drops below 35 degrees C (normal range 36.5--37.5 degrees C)
- Hebrew term: **היפותרמיה** or **מכת קור** (cold strike)

### Heat Loss Mechanisms
- Evaporation (אידוי)
- Radiation (קרינה)
- Conduction (הולכה)
- Convection (הסעה)

### Treatment Principles (from MDA training)
1. Remove from cold environment
2. Remove wet clothing
3. Dry patient and wrap in blankets
4. Perform ABC assessment
5. Warm head, neck, chest, and groin
6. Transport urgently to hospital
7. Do NOT give alcohol, caffeine, or allow smoking
8. Minimise movement (risk of arrhythmia in cold heart)

### Paediatric Considerations (from MDA course materials)
- MDA training courses for infant/child resuscitation explicitly cover hypothermia prevention
- Procedures for children described as "identical to adults but performed with much greater gentleness"
- Infants and elderly listed as high-risk populations for hypothermia
- Infants have immature thermoregulation systems
- Children have higher surface-area-to-body-mass ratio, accelerating heat loss

## Child-Specific Clinical Notes (from MDA training context)
- Infants may NOT shiver -- absence of shivering does not rule out hypothermia
- Infants are obligate nasal breathers initially; nasal patency must be maintained
- Infants utilise approximately 95% of oxygen supply (vs 50% in adults)
- Smaller body mass means faster cooling
- Cover infant/child thoroughly during any outdoor assessment to prevent hypothermia
