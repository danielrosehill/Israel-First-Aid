## General MDA Recovery Position Principles

The recovery position (תנוחת התאוששות) is used for unconscious patients who are breathing normally and have no suspected spinal injury. Its purpose is to:
- Reduce risk of airway obstruction
- Facilitate drainage of airway secretions
- Reduce risk of aspiration

### Standard Recovery Position (Adults and Children over 1 year)
1. Kneel beside the patient
2. Place the arm nearest to you at a right angle to the body
3. Bring the far arm across the chest and hold the back of their hand against the nearest cheek
4. Bend the far knee and roll the patient towards you
5. Adjust the upper leg so hip and knee are at right angles
6. Tilt the head back to keep the airway open
7. Monitor breathing continuously

### Infant Recovery Position (under 1 year)
- Infants should be held in arms in a head-down, face-down position (supported on the rescuer's forearm)
- This allows drainage of fluids from the airway
- Keep the infant's head lower than the body
- Support the head and jaw with one hand
- Do NOT use the standard lateral recovery position for small infants

## Child-Specific Considerations (from MDA training context)
- Children aged 1--8: use the same recovery position as adults, performed more gently
- Infants (under 1 year): use the cradle/arm position described above
- Children have proportionally larger heads -- ensure head position does not flex the neck and obstruct the airway
- In MDA's training: "procedures for children are identical to adults but performed with much greater gentleness"
- Always check and maintain airway patency
- Ensure proper coverage to prevent hypothermia while in recovery position
