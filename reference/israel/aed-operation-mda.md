## When to Use an AED

Use an AED on any person who is:

1. **Unresponsive** -- does not respond to calling or shaking
2. **Not breathing normally** -- not breathing at all, or only gasping (agonal breathing)
3. **Suspected cardiac arrest** -- no pulse detected (if trained to check)

The AED will analyse the heart rhythm and will ONLY deliver a shock if it detects a shockable rhythm (ventricular fibrillation or pulseless ventricular tachycardia). The device makes this decision -- the operator does not need to decide whether to shock.

### When NOT to Use an AED

- **The person is conscious and breathing normally** -- an AED is only for cardiac arrest
- **The person is submerged in water** -- move them to dry ground first before applying pads
- **Flammable environment** -- do not use near flammable gases or oxygen-enriched atmospheres
- **The person is breathing and has a pulse** -- even if unconscious, do not apply AED; place in recovery position and monitor

**Important**: An AED cannot harm a person who does not need a shock. If the rhythm is not shockable, the device will not deliver a shock and will instruct the operator to continue CPR.

## AED Operation Protocol -- Adults

### Step 1: Recognise Cardiac Arrest and Call 101

1. Check responsiveness -- call out, shake shoulders
2. Check breathing -- look, listen, feel for no more than 10 seconds
3. If unresponsive and not breathing normally: **Call 101 immediately**
4. Put MDA dispatch on speakerphone -- they will guide you through the process
5. If someone else is present, send them to fetch the nearest AED while you begin CPR

### Step 2: Begin CPR Immediately

1. Place the person on a firm, flat surface
2. Begin chest compressions:
   - Place the heel of one hand on the centre of the chest (lower half of the sternum)
   - Place the other hand on top, interlace fingers
   - Compress to a depth of **5-6 cm** (2-2.4 inches)
   - Rate: **100-120 compressions per minute**
   - Allow full chest recoil between compressions
3. If trained: give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions (30:2 ratio)
4. If untrained or unwilling: perform compression-only CPR

### Step 3: Retrieve and Turn On the AED

1. Locate the nearest AED:
   - Use the **Eifo-Defi** app to find the closest device
   - Call 101 -- MDA dispatch can locate the nearest smart stand and remotely unlock it
   - Check nearby buildings, shopping centres, train stations, sports facilities
2. **Open the AED cover** -- the device will power on automatically (some models require pressing a power button)
3. The AED will begin providing **voice instructions in Hebrew** (and other languages depending on the model)

### Step 4: Prepare the Patient and Apply Pads

1. **Expose the bare chest** -- remove or cut away clothing, including bra
2. **Ensure the chest is dry** -- wipe off sweat, water, or other moisture
3. **Check for and address special situations**:
   - **Excessive chest hair**: if pads will not stick, shave the area using the razor included in many AED kits
   - **Medication patches**: remove any transdermal patches from the chest area and wipe the skin
   - **Implanted pacemaker/defibrillator** (visible lump under the skin): place pads at least 2.5 cm (1 inch) away from the device
   - **Metal surface**: move the person off the metal surface if possible, or ensure you are not in contact with the metal when the shock is delivered
4. **Apply the electrode pads** following the diagram printed on each pad:
   - **Pad 1 (upper right)**: below the right collarbone, to the right of the sternum
   - **Pad 2 (lower left)**: on the left side of the chest, below the armpit, in line with the nipple
   - This is the **anterolateral** placement (standard for adults)
5. **Connect the pads to the AED** if not pre-connected (plug the cable into the AED)

### Step 5: Allow the AED to Analyse

1. The AED will instruct: "Do not touch the patient" / "Analysing heart rhythm"
2. **Stop CPR and ensure nobody is touching the patient** during analysis
3. The AED will determine if a shock is needed

### Step 6: Deliver Shock (If Advised)

1. If the AED says "Shock advised":
   - Ensure nobody is touching the patient
   - Call out clearly: **"Stand clear!"** (Hebrew: "Hitrachku!")
   - Press the **shock button** when instructed (some fully automatic AEDs deliver the shock without pressing a button)
2. If the AED says "No shock advised":
   - Resume CPR immediately (30:2 or compression-only)

### Step 7: Resume CPR

1. **Immediately after the shock** (or "no shock" advisory), resume CPR
2. Continue for **2 minutes** (approximately 5 cycles of 30:2)
3. The AED will automatically re-analyse after 2 minutes
4. **Do not turn off the AED** -- leave pads attached at all times

### Step 8: Continue Until Help Arrives

1. Continue the cycle of: CPR -> AED analysis -> shock if advised -> CPR
2. **Do not stop** until:
   - MDA paramedics arrive and take over
   - The person shows obvious signs of life (moves, breathes normally, opens eyes)
   - You are physically unable to continue
3. **Do not disconnect the AED pads** even if the person regains consciousness

## AED Operation Protocol -- Children and Infants

### Age Definitions (per MDA / AHA guidelines)

| Category | Age |
|----------|-----|
| **Adult** | Age 8 and above (or puberty signs) |
| **Child** | Age 1 to 8 years |
| **Infant** | Under 1 year |

### Paediatric AED Use

The protocol sequence is the same as for adults (recognise -> call 101 -> CPR -> AED), with these critical modifications:

#### Pad Selection

- **Children under 8 years / under 25 kg**: Use **paediatric pads** (reduced energy dose) if available
- **If paediatric pads are not available**: Use adult pads -- **do not delay defibrillation** because paediatric pads are unavailable
- **Infants (under 1 year)**: Use paediatric pads if available; adult pads if not. A manual defibrillator is preferred for infants but an AED should be used if that is what is available

#### Paediatric Mode

Many AEDs in Israel have a **paediatric mode switch** (a button or key that switches the device to deliver a lower energy dose appropriate for children). Activate this before applying pads if the patient is a child under 8.

MDA notes: "The device has a switch to child mode and/or pad replacement depending on the model" (Hebrew: "כפתור מעבר למצב ילדים ו/או החלפת מדבקות בהתאם לדגם").

#### Pad Placement -- Children and Infants

For children under 8 and infants, pad placement differs from adults:

- **Anteroposterior (front-and-back) placement** is preferred for small children and infants:
  - **Front pad**: centre of the chest (sternum), at the level of the heart
  - **Back pad**: centre of the back, between the shoulder blades, directly behind the front pad
  - This placement is used because the child's chest is too small for both pads to be placed on the front without touching each other

- **Anterolateral placement** (same as adults) may be used for larger children if the pads can be placed at least 2.5 cm apart and do not overlap

**Critical rule**: Pads must NEVER touch or overlap each other. If the child's chest is too small for anterolateral placement, always use anteroposterior placement.

#### CPR Modifications for Children

- **Compression depth**: approximately one-third of the chest depth (about 5 cm / 2 inches for children, 4 cm / 1.5 inches for infants)
- **Compression technique for infants**: use two fingers (or two-thumb encircling technique if two rescuers)
- **Ratio**: 30:2 for single rescuer; 15:2 for two trained rescuers (children and infants)
- **For children**: if alone, perform 2 minutes of CPR before calling 101 (cardiac arrest in children is more often respiratory in origin)

## Israeli AED Infrastructure

### Smart Stand Programme

MDA operates a national network of **"smart stand"** AED stations across Israel, developed in partnership with Pointer by Powerfleet (IoT technology).

**How smart stands work:**

1. When a cardiac arrest call comes in to MDA's 101 Emergency Dispatch Centre, the dispatcher identifies the nearest smart stand AED
2. The dispatcher **remotely unlocks** the stand -- the case opens and a **siren and flashing lights** activate to help the caller locate it
3. The AED is tracked in real time by the dispatch centre
4. The dispatcher provides real-time CPR and AED guidance over the phone
5. Simultaneously, an ambulance is dispatched to the scene
6. After use, MDA staff retrieve and service the device

**Deployment numbers:**

- Over **1,000 smart stands** deployed nationwide as of 2022 (with expansion ongoing via 800+ additional units)
- Locations include: shopping centres, public transport hubs, sports facilities, public halls, government buildings, entrances to large buildings, parks, and rural communities
- Some smart stands were created by converting obsolete **Bezeq public phone booths** into AED stations -- bright yellow boxes with red lettering

**Technical features:**

- IoT-connected via SIM card
- Self-diagnostic capability -- alerts MDA technicians to malfunctions
- Temperature-control sensors to prevent overheating in Israeli summer conditions
- Vandalism-resistant, weather-protected cabinets
- Remote unlock capability by dispatch centre
- Siren and flashing lights upon activation

### Eifo-Defi App

**"Eifo Defi"** (Hebrew: "Where's the Defib?" / "איפה דפי") is a crowdsourced AED locator app:

- Shows the location of MDA's defibrillators and other registered AEDs in the user's area
- Users can add AED locations they discover that are not yet in the database
- Locations are verified by MDA and integrated into the national dispatch database
- MDA maintains a centralised database of **tens of thousands of AEDs** across the country
- Available on Google Play and the App Store

### MDA National AED Database

MDA maintains a centralized database incorporating tens of thousands of AEDs across Israel. The system integrates data from the Eifo-Defi app, partner organisations, and legally mandated AED registrations. This database is accessible to MDA's 101 dispatch centre, enabling dispatchers to direct callers to the nearest available AED.

## Israeli AED Legislation

### Law on Placement of Resuscitation Devices in Public Places (2008)

Israel enacted the **"Law on Placement of Resuscitation Devices in Public Places"** (Hebrew: "חוק הצבת מכשירי החייאה במקומות ציבוריים") in 2008, with implementing regulations in 2014.

### Who Must Have an AED

The following venues are **legally required** to maintain an AED:

| Venue Type | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Government offices receiving the public | 500+ daily visitors |
| Local authority offices | 500+ daily visitors |
| Sports stadiums | 500+ seats |
| Fitness centres | 500+ regular members |
| Event halls, gardens, conference centres | Licensed for 500+ guests |
| Higher education institutions | 500+ students |
| Schools | 500+ students |
| Workplaces | 500+ employees |
| Hotels | 250+ rooms |
| Central bus stations | -- |
| Train stations | 500+ daily passengers |
| Airports and ports | -- |
| Ships | 100+ passengers |
| Aircraft | 100+ seats |
| Assisted living facilities and nursing homes | -- |
| Detention facilities and prisons | -- |
| Public swimming pools | -- |
| Designated beaches | -- |

### Placement Requirements

- At least **one device per two adjacent floors** where daily visitors may total 500+
- For floors exceeding **300 metres in length** with 500+ visitors: multiple units spaced no more than 300 metres apart
- Device must be placed in a **central and accessible area** in a fixed cabinet that can be easily opened
- Cabinet positioned between **120-150 cm** from the ground
- **Directional signs** pointing to the AED must be placed in prominent locations
- Signs on each floor spaced no more than 150 metres apart
- A **designated employee** must verify device functionality and ensure signage compliance

### Education Mandate

A 2018 amendment requires **10th-grade students** nationwide to receive first aid training that includes hands-on AED practice.

## Common AED Features in Israeli Devices

Based on MDA's defibrillator information page, AEDs available in Israel typically include:

- **Automatic heart rhythm analysis during compressions** -- some models can analyse while CPR continues
- **Alerts when the device is not ready for use** -- self-diagnostic warnings
- **Paediatric mode switch** -- button or key to switch to child mode, and/or separate paediatric pad sets
- **Audible metronome** -- guides compression rate
- **Visual and audio prompts** -- step-by-step guidance throughout the resuscitation

### AED Brands in Israel

- Hands-on CPR and AED practice sessions for all ages

MDA offers AED training as part of their broader first aid and CPR courses:

- **EMT training** -- includes BLS with AED as a core competency
- **Public first aid courses** -- include AED familiarisation
- **School programmes** -- 10th-grade mandatory first aid training includes AED use (per 2018 law amendment)
- MDA emphasises that **no prior medical knowledge is required** to use an AED -- the device guides the user

## Integration with CPR Protocol

The AED is a component of MDA's **"Chain of Survival"** (Hebrew: "שרשרת ההישרדות"):

1. **Early recognition** -- identify cardiac arrest
2. **Early call** -- dial 101
3. **Early CPR** -- begin chest compressions immediately
4. **Early defibrillation** -- use AED as soon as available
5. **Early advanced care** -- MDA paramedics and hospital treatment

**Critical timing**: MDA emphasises that the **first 4 minutes** are critical. Brain damage begins after approximately 4-6 minutes without blood flow. For every minute that defibrillation is delayed, survival decreases by approximately 7-10%.

Israel experiences approximately **8,000 cardiac arrests annually**, with roughly half occurring outside hospitals.

### Programme Outcomes (Smart Stand Programme)

From the smart stand programme tracking data:

- Over 80 smart stand activations by the public
- 43 cases correctly identified as cardiac arrest by bystanders
- 10 cases presented shockable rhythms with pre-EMS defibrillation
- 4 patients recovered with no neurological damage
