## GAP FLAG

**No specific hypothermia/cold exposure protocol was found published on United Hatzalah's website (1221.org.il) or on Hatzalah Without Borders (hatzalah.org.il).**

United Hatzalah's publicly available first aid content focuses primarily on:
- CPR/resuscitation (adults and infants)
- Haemorrhage control
- Fracture stabilisation
- Choking

Hatzalah Without Borders publishes life-saving guides on their website covering topics such as eye injuries, animal bites, diarrhoea, electrocution, burns, and dehydration — but no standalone hypothermia guide was found.

## What Is Known

United Hatzalah's EMT course (NREMT-level, offered in English in Jerusalem) would cover hypothermia as part of the standard curriculum. Their basic first aid course (offered free during wartime as a public service) covers adult/infant CPR, wound management, and bleeding control, but does not appear to include hypothermia in its publicly listed curriculum.

United Hatzalah volunteers carry medic bags with thermal blankets (space blankets / "sdinat aluminium"), which are standard equipment for hypothermia management in the field.

## Recommendation

For Israeli hypothermia protocols, rely on MDA guidelines (see hypothermia-mda.md). United Hatzalah's operational protocols are internal and not published for public consumption. Their training aligns with Israeli Ministry of Health standards, which are administered through MDA.
