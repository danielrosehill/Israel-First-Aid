## Availability of Child-Specific Protocol

**GAP IDENTIFIED**: MDA does not publish a dedicated child-specific tourniquet protocol on their public website. MDA training courses include tourniquet application but paediatric size/age guidance is not available in publicly accessible materials.

## General MDA Tourniquet Guidance

### Context
MDA has incorporated tourniquet training into standard first aid courses, reflecting Israel's security environment. Tourniquets are part of civilian emergency preparedness.

### When to Apply
- Uncontrollable limb haemorrhage (life-threatening bleeding)
- Amputation or near-amputation injuries
- Blast/shrapnel injuries with arterial bleeding
- When direct pressure is insufficient or impractical

### Application Principles (from MDA training)
1. Apply tourniquet proximal (closer to body) to the wound
2. Tighten until bleeding stops
3. Record the time of application
4. Do NOT remove once applied -- hospital personnel will manage removal
5. Transport urgently to hospital

### Equipment
- MDA and Israeli emergency services commonly use the **CAT (Combat Application Tourniquet)**
- Ichud Hatzalah shop sells CAT SAM XT (original) and CAT-compatible tourniquets

## Child-Specific Tourniquet Information

### From International Evidence (applicable to Israeli training)

**CAT Gen 7 Tourniquet**
- Effective for most school-age children
- Success demonstrated in limbs with upper-extremity circumferences of approximately 16 cm or greater
- Some sources cite effective occlusion down to approximately 13 cm circumference
- Good first choice for children aged approximately 2 years and above (depending on limb size)
- **FAILED on smallest paediatric mannequin** (5.08 cm diameter) in 2018 NAEMSP study

**Children Under 2 Years / Very Small Limbs**
- No manufactured windlass tourniquet is reliably effective on very small limbs
- **SWAT-T** (elastic wrap tourniquet) and **RATS** tourniquet showed success across all paediatric mannequin sizes in testing
- For infants: direct pressure and wound packing are preferred over tourniquet
- Improvised tourniquet (triangular bandage + windlass) may be necessary

**Alternative Paediatric Tourniquets**
- **SWAT-T**: Elastic, stretch-wrap design; effective on all sizes including very small limbs
- **RATS**: Ratcheting design; successful across all paediatric sizes
- **RMT M2 Pediatric (yellow)**: Purpose-built for children; marketed to work down to approximately 6.4 cm / 2.5 inches circumference and under 120 lb

### Limb Circumference Guide (approximate)
- CAT Gen 7: effective from approximately 13--16 cm circumference and above
- SWAT-T/RATS: effective from smallest paediatric sizes upward
- Below 13 cm circumference: use elastic-type tourniquets or direct pressure

### Key Paediatric Principles
- Apply with same urgency as in adults -- children have less blood volume and can decompensate rapidly
- Apply tourniquet 5--7 cm (2--3 inches) proximal to wound
- Tighten until distal bleeding stops
- Record time of application clearly
- Re-assess frequently
- Consider pain management -- tourniquet application is extremely painful

## Gaps and Limitations
- MDA does not publish paediatric tourniquet protocols publicly
- No MDA-specific guidance on which tourniquet models to use for children
- MDA training course content on paediatric tourniquets is not available online
- Evidence base for paediatric tourniquets comes primarily from NAEMSP and TCCC studies, not Israeli-specific research
