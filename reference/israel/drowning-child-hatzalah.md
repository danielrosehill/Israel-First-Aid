## United Hatzalah -- Pediatric CPR Protocol (Applicable to Drowning)

### Age Definitions

| Term | Age Range |
|---|---|
| Newborn | Birth to 1 month |
| Infant | 1 month to 1 year |
| Child | 1 year to puberty onset (approximately ages 10-14) |

### Step 1: Check Consciousness (30-Second Assessment)

- For infants: use verbal stimulation and familiar sounds.
- For children: firmly tap the shoulder and call out loudly.
- Assess breathing by observing chest and abdominal movement (do not evaluate depth or regularity -- just determine if breathing is present).

### Step 2: Call for Help

- If a second person is present, have them call **1221** (United Hatzalah) or **101** (MDA) immediately.
- If alone, perform 2 minutes of CPR (5 cycles) before calling for help (pediatric protocol).

### Step 3: Check Pulse

- Palpate the **brachial artery** (inner upper arm) or **femoral artery** (groin) in children and infants.
- Begin compressions if:
  - No pulse is detected, OR
  - Pulse is below 60 bpm with signs of poor perfusion.

### Step 4: Chest Compressions

#### Technique by Age

| Scenario | Technique |
|---|---|
| Child, single rescuer | Heel of one hand on lower third of sternum |
| Infant, single rescuer | Two-finger technique on sternum, just below the nipple line |
| Infant, two rescuers | Encircling technique -- hands wrap around chest, thumbs compress sternum |

#### Compression Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Depth | One-third to one-half the depth of the chest |
| Rate | 100-120 compressions per minute |
| Recoil | Allow full chest recoil between compressions |
| Interruptions | Minimise; avoid pauses longer than 10 seconds |
| Rotation | Switch rescuers every 2 minutes (5 cycles) to prevent fatigue |

#### Key Warnings

- Avoid compressing over the xiphoid process (bottom tip of sternum).
- Allow full chest recoil -- do not lean on the chest between compressions.

### Step 5: Rescue Breathing

- Provide gentle ventilation lasting approximately **1 second per breath**.
- Produce **visible chest rise** with each breath.
- Avoid excessive force or volume to prevent gastric distention (stomach inflation).
- For infants: cover both mouth and nose with your mouth.
- For children: pinch nose, seal mouth over child's mouth.

### Step 6: Compression-to-Ventilation Ratios

| Scenario | Ratio |
|---|---|
| Single rescuer (child or infant) | 30 compressions : 2 breaths |
| Two rescuers (child or infant) | 15 compressions : 2 breaths |
| Newborn | 3 compressions : 1 breath (120 compressions/min) |
| With advanced airway in place | 100-120 compressions/min continuous + 8-10 breaths/min |

### Step 7: Recovery

- If spontaneous breathing resumes, place the child on their side in the recovery position.
- Maintain warmth during resuscitation -- infants and children are particularly vulnerable to hypothermia.
- Continue monitoring until EMS arrives.

## Drowning-Specific Notes from United Hatzalah Sources

The following drowning-specific guidance was gathered from United Hatzalah course descriptions and associated materials:

1. **Rescue breaths are essential** in drowning -- compression-only CPR is insufficient because drowning is primarily a respiratory (oxygen-deprivation) emergency.
2. **Do not attempt to remove water from the lungs** -- this is ineffective and dangerous.
3. Children under 5 are at highest risk, particularly in domestic pools and bathtubs.
4. Drowning can occur in very shallow water -- infants can drown in sinks, buckets, or bathtubs.

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

United Hatzalah's pediatric CPR protocol is **fully consistent** with MDA's published guidelines on:

- Compression-to-ventilation ratios (30:2 solo, 15:2 two rescuers)
- Compression rate (100-120/min)
- Compression depth (1/3 to 1/2 chest depth)
- Rescue breathing technique and emphasis
- Recovery position guidance

No material differences were identified between the two organisations' publicly available guidance for pediatric drowning response.

## Gaps and Limitations

1. **No standalone drowning protocol** is published by United Hatzalah on their website. The protocol above is their general pediatric CPR protocol applied to the drowning context.
2. **The Hatzalah Without Borders drowning guide** (hatzalah.org.il) was not fully accessible at time of research -- the page loaded but article body content could not be extracted.
3. **Course-specific drowning training content** (from United Hatzalah's "Safe Family" course) is not published online and would require attending the course.
4. **In-water rescue** guidance was not found in United Hatzalah's public materials.
