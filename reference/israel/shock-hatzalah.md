## Findings

### Gap: No Publicly Available Shock Protocol

United Hatzalah does not publish specific medical treatment protocols for shock (or other conditions) on their public-facing websites. Their online presence focuses on:
- Organisational mission and fundraising
- Volunteer recruitment
- Their psychotrauma and crisis response unit
- First aid course registration (via shop.1221.org.il)

### What Was Found

1. **Psychotrauma Unit**: United Hatzalah has extensive public content about their Psychotrauma and Crisis Response Unit, which treats psychological/emotional shock at emergency scenes. This is distinct from medical (circulatory) shock.

2. **First Aid Courses**: United Hatzalah offers a 44-hour first aid course (קורס מגיש עזרה ראשונה) through their online shop, which presumably covers shock treatment. However, the course content/curriculum is not published online.

3. **Volunteer Training**: All United Hatzalah volunteers undergo training that includes medical emergency response, but their specific medical protocols are internal training materials, not publicly accessible documents.

### Relationship to MDA Protocols

United Hatzalah volunteers operate under the Israeli Ministry of Health's emergency medical regulations. Their first aid training is standardised to align with MDA-approved curricula (as MDA is the statutory authority for emergency medical services in Israel). Therefore, shock treatment protocols used by United Hatzalah volunteers are expected to be consistent with MDA guidelines.

## Recommendation

For the purposes of this project, the MDA shock protocol (see `shock-mda.md`) should be treated as the authoritative Israeli protocol. United Hatzalah's medical treatment protocols are not publicly available and would need to be obtained through direct contact with the organisation or through their training courses.
