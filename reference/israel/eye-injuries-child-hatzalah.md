## Eye Injury Protocol (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### Types of Eye Burns

**Thermal Burns**
- Rare due to natural eyelid protection (blink reflex)
- Occur from direct flame exposure
- Cause severe damage to facial skin and eyelids

**UV Radiation Burns**
- Common from unprotected welding, prolonged snow/sea exposure
- Symptoms appear hours later: blurred vision, eye pain, light flashes
- Requires urgent hospital referral for assessment with antibiotics, pupil-dilating drops, and sometimes steroids

**Chemical Burns**
- Most severe type of eye injury
- Both acidic and basic substances damage the corneal surface
- Treatment: **15--30 minute irrigation with flowing sterile water** before urgent hospital transport
- Must obtain the chemical's MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet)

### Penetrating Eye Injuries
- Common causes: knives, pens, projectiles, metal fragments from workplace or combat incidents
- Deep penetration risks lens opacity, retinal damage, permanent vision loss
- **Management**:
  - Avoid any pressure on the eye
  - Do NOT apply bandages (pressure on open eye worsens wound)
  - Use rigid plastic eye shield without contact
  - Position patient supine during transport
  - Transport to nearest hospital with on-call ophthalmologist

### Foreign Objects
- Small particles (wood, metal) may self-flush
- If embedded, cover with sterile gauze and refer for evaluation
- Do NOT apply pressure, scratch, or rub the eye

### Blunt Eye Trauma
- Internal bleeding presents as dark circles around the eye
- In elderly patients on anticoagulants or head trauma cases, "raccoon eyes" sign indicates serious head injury

## Eye Injury Protocol (from Hatzalah Without Borders)

### Chemical Exposure
- Rush to nearest faucet and rinse with plenty of water
- Simultaneously assess whether medical help is needed
- Rinse injured eye with stream of water for **15 minutes to 30 minutes**
- Then seek treatment at nearest emergency room

### Foreign Object Penetration
- Rinse eye using flowing lukewarm water for about 15 minutes
- Even after object is dislodged, see ophthalmologist to check for remaining injury

## Child-Specific Considerations

### Foreign Objects in Nose (related -- from same Ichud Hatzalah page)
- Common in young children: batteries, small toys, magnetic objects, leaves
- Keep child calm, breathing through mouth
- Do NOT attempt extraction
- Prevent sneezing/coughing to avoid aspiration
- Transport to emergency department

### Paediatric Eye Injury Context for Israel
- Playground injuries (sand, sticks, swings) are common causes in children
- Household chemical products (cleaning agents) accessible to children are a frequent cause of chemical eye burns
- Children may be unable to cooperate with prolonged eye irrigation -- may need assistance from two adults (one to hold child, one to irrigate)
- Always explain procedures to the child honestly

## Gaps and Limitations
- Neither Ichud Hatzalah nor Hatzalah Without Borders publish a dedicated child-specific eye injury protocol
- Paediatric eye injury information is not separated from adult guidance
- No specific dosing or technique modifications for children documented
