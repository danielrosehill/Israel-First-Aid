## Types of Eye Injuries

### 1. Foreign Body in the Eye (Guf Zar)
- Dust, sand, metal shavings, wood chips

### 2. Chemical Splash (Chomer Khimi)
- Acids, alkalis, cleaning products, industrial chemicals

### 3. Embedded / Penetrating Objects
- Glass, metal, wood splinters that are lodged in the eye

### 4. Blunt Trauma
- Punch, ball impact, airbag deployment, falls

### 5. Cuts and Lacerations
- Sharp objects cutting the eyelid or eyeball surface

## First Aid by Injury Type

### Foreign Body (Non-Embedded)
1. **Do NOT rub the eye** — this can cause further damage
2. Rinse the eye with lukewarm running water for approximately 15 minutes
3. Check every 5 minutes whether the foreign body has been dislodged
4. If the object does not come out with flushing, seek medical attention
5. If symptoms persist after removal (pain, redness, tearing), go to an emergency room

### Chemical Splash
1. **Flush immediately** — rinse the affected eye with a stream of running water for 15 to 30 minutes
2. Hold the eyelid open during flushing to ensure the chemical is washed out
3. Remove contact lenses if present (after starting the flush)
4. **After flushing, take the casualty to an emergency room (Khadar Miyun)**
5. If possible, bring the chemical container or note the product name for the medical team
6. **Call MDA at 101** for severe chemical burns or if the person cannot open their eye

### Embedded / Penetrating Object
1. **Do NOT attempt to remove the object** — this is critical
2. **Do NOT apply pressure** to the eye or the object
3. Cover BOTH eyes loosely (covering both prevents sympathetic eye movement)
4. Use a cup, cone, or ring of cloth around the object to protect it from being pushed further
5. Keep the casualty calm and still
6. **Call MDA at 101 immediately** — this requires hospital treatment

### Blunt Trauma
1. Apply a cold compress gently (do NOT press on the eyeball)
2. If vision is affected, call MDA at 101
3. Watch for signs of serious injury: double vision, blood in the eye, irregular pupil shape, severe pain
4. Seek medical evaluation even if the eye looks normal — internal damage may not be visible

### Cuts to the Eye or Eyelid
1. **Do NOT wash the eye** if there is a laceration to the eyeball
2. Cover loosely with a sterile pad without applying pressure
3. **Call MDA at 101** — this requires emergency room treatment
4. Keep the casualty lying down if possible

## When to Call MDA (101) Immediately

- Any penetrating injury
- Chemical burns (after initiating flushing)
- Sudden loss of vision or significant vision change
- Blood visible in the eye
- Severe pain that does not resolve
- Object embedded in the eye
- Laceration of the eyeball

## Israeli Context

- **Construction and agriculture**: Common workplace eye injuries from dust, chemicals, metal/wood fragments. Israeli labour safety regulations (Takanot Betihut ba-Avodah) require eye protection in these settings.
- **Military/security**: Blast injuries can cause eye trauma; shrapnel fragments
- **Desert environment**: Sand and dust storms (khamsin) can cause foreign body injuries
- **Swimming**: Chemical irritation from pool chlorination; Dead Sea salt/mineral exposure
- **Industrial chemicals**: Agricultural pesticides, cleaning agents in commercial settings
