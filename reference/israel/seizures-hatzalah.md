### Notes on source access

United Hatzalah does not publish a standalone public-facing seizure first aid protocol document on their website. Their seizure management guidance is delivered through:

1. **Basic first aid course** (קורס עזרה ראשונה בסיסי) -- covers "treatment of the injured" and emergency response; seizures are part of the curriculum but specific protocol steps are not published online
2. **44-hour EMT certification course** -- comprehensive training that includes seizure management
3. **Online learning platform** at `school.1221.org.il` (Moodle-based)
4. **Case studies / field stories** published on israelrescue.org -- these describe how United Hatzalah volunteers manage seizure calls in practice

The protocol details below are derived from the available case study material and from United Hatzalah's course descriptions. Where detailed step-by-step protocol was not available from United Hatzalah directly, this is noted.

## Protocol Summary (from available sources)

Based on United Hatzalah case study material (EMT responding to a seizure on an aircraft), the following protocol elements were documented:

### During the seizure:

1. **Secure the area around the patient** -- clear objects and hazards to prevent injuries during convulsions
2. **Do not restrain the person** -- let the seizure run its course
3. **Wait for the seizure to conclude** -- standard protocol is to allow the seizure to end naturally

### After the seizure:

1. **Assess vital signs** once the seizure subsides and the patient begins to regain consciousness
2. **Administer oxygen** if available and trained to do so
3. **Monitor the patient** for additional seizures or deterioration
4. **Arrange transport to medical facility** if needed

### When to dispatch emergency response:

- United Hatzalah volunteers are dispatched via the NowForce GPS-based dispatch system when a call comes in to 1221
- Average response time: approximately 90 seconds
- Volunteers carry first aid kits that include equipment for managing unconsciousness and airway management

Seizure management is included in their training curriculum but the specific protocol steps taught in the course are not published publicly online.

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

United Hatzalah's approach to seizure management, as evidenced by their case study material, is consistent with the MDA protocol:

- Secure the scene and prevent injury
- Do not restrain the person
- Wait for the seizure to end
- Assess and monitor after the seizure
- Call for additional emergency support if needed

No significant divergence between United Hatzalah and MDA protocols was identified for seizure management. Both organisations follow the same fundamental approach: protect, do not intervene during the seizure, position safely, and call for help when indicated.

## Gaps and Limitations

1. **No standalone seizure protocol published online.** United Hatzalah does not publish a detailed step-by-step seizure first aid guide on their public website (neither israelrescue.org nor 1221.org.il).
2. **Protocol details limited to case study descriptions.** The specific first aid steps documented above are derived from a single published case study of a United Hatzalah EMT responding to a seizure, rather than from a formal protocol document.
3. **Course content not publicly available.** The detailed seizure management protocol taught in United Hatzalah's first aid courses is not available online -- it is delivered in-person or through their restricted online learning platform.
4. **Recommend cross-referencing with MDA protocol.** For a complete seizure first aid protocol in the Israeli context, refer to `seizures-mda.md` which provides a more detailed, corroborated protocol from multiple authoritative Israeli medical sources.
