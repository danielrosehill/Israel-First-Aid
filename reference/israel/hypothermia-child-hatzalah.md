## Hypothermia -- General Protocol (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### Definition
Hypothermia is a progressive condition divided into 4 stages, occurring when body temperature drops below normal (36.5--37.5 degrees C). Hebrew: **היפותרמיה** or **מכת קור**.

### Four Severity Stages

**Stage 1 -- Frost Nip / Shivering (37--35 degrees C)**
- Warning condition; uncomfortable but not yet dangerous
- No impairment to judgement
- Shivering appears as body attempts self-warming

**Stage 2 -- Mild Hypothermia (35--32 degrees C)**
- Severe shivering
- Memory problems, slurred speech, confusion
- Possible impaired judgement
- Elevated heart and respiratory rates
- Skin pallor, fingertip discolouration
- Increased urination

**Stage 3 -- Moderate Hypothermia (32--28 degrees C)**
- Deteriorating consciousness, possible hallucinations or unconsciousness
- Weakening reflexes, dilated pupils with poor light response
- Loss of cough reflex
- Paradoxical undressing (removing clothes due to confusion)
- Bradycardia, low blood pressure, cardiac arrhythmias
- Muscle rigidity

**Stage 4 -- Severe Hypothermia (below 28 degrees C)**
- Unconsciousness, absent neurological reflexes
- Severely slowed breathing or apnoea
- Pulmonary oedema
- Extreme bradycardia or asystole
- Ventricular fibrillation risk
- Severe muscle rigidity resembling rigor mortis

### Risk Factors
- Limited mobility or inability to move freely
- **Infants** (explicitly listed)
- Elderly
- Psychiatric conditions
- Alcohol and drug use
- Homelessness
- Inadequate hypothalamic function

### First Aid Treatment
1. Remove patient from cold environment
2. Remove wet clothing
3. Dry and position horizontally
4. Cover patient with warmed blankets
5. Perform ABC assessment
6. Monitor heart rate carefully (cold heart is sensitive to movement)
7. Apply warmed blankets to head, neck, chest, and groin
8. Consider heated IV fluids (never cold fluids)
9. Transport to hospital urgently

**Key principle**: "No one is dead until they are warm and dead" -- severe hypothermia patients may appear deceased but can recover with proper rewarming.

### Contraindications
- No alcohol, caffeine, or smoking
- Avoid excessive movement (may trigger arrhythmias)
- Minimise jostling during transport

## Child-Specific Information (from Pediatric pages)

From the Ichud Hatzalah pediatric treatment approach page:

- **Infants face particular risk** due to underdeveloped thermoregulation, immobility, and high surface-area-to-volume ratio
- Children lose heat rapidly -- must ensure proper coverage during any medical assessment
- When providing first aid to children, ensure proper coverage to prevent hypothermia (mentioned as a standard step in all pediatric emergencies)
- Emergency situations in children page lists hypothermia alongside other pediatric emergencies (shivering, slurred speech, coordination loss, progressive organ dysfunction)
- Treatment mirrors adult protocols: airway protection, oxygen, gentle handling, gradual rewarming

### Age Classifications (from Ichud Hatzalah)
- Newborns: birth to 4 weeks
- Infants: 3 weeks to 1 year
- Children: 1--8 years
- Adults: 8+ years

### Vital Signs Ranges
- Under 1 year: 30--40 breaths/min, 110--160 bpm, 70--90 mmHg systolic
- 1--2 years: 25--35 breaths/min, 100--150 bpm, 80--95 mmHg systolic
- 2--5 years: 25--30 breaths/min, 95--140 bpm, 80--100 mmHg systolic
