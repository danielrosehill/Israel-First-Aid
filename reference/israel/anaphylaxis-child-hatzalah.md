## Overview

United Hatzalah's anaphylaxis protocol for children follows the same framework as their adult protocol, with specific paediatric modifications. Their 1221.org.il educational page on anaphylaxis covers both adults and children in a single protocol with paediatric notes embedded.

## Key Paediatric Points from United Hatzalah Sources

### EpiPen Campaign
United Hatzalah launched an initiative to equip all volunteers (8,000+) with epinephrine auto-injectors. Their EpiPen kits each contain **two pens: one for a child and one for an adult**, confirming that paediatric dosing is part of their standard field kit.

**Context on availability:** EpiPens are expensive in Israel and have a short shelf life (approximately 15-18 months from production). Prior to the campaign, only a handful of medics carried EpiPens despite receiving 4,586 calls relating to anaphylactic emergencies in 2019 alone.

### Dosing (Children)
United Hatzalah uses the same weight-based dosing as MDA:

| Weight | Dose |
|--------|------|
| Up to 10 kg | 0.01 mg/kg |
| 10-25 kg | 0.15 mg |
| 25-50 kg | 0.3 mg |
| Over 50 kg | 0.5 mg |

### Administration Technique -- Child-Specific
From the 1221.org.il anaphylaxis page:

- Same technique as for adults (remove safety cap, 90 degrees to outer thigh, hold firmly for 10 seconds)
- **For children: "Gently pinch the thigh before injection to avoid bone contact, especially in small/thin children"** -- this is the key paediatric modification
- Can be administered through clothing
- Reassess within 5 minutes; a second dose may be administered if no improvement

### Case Reports Involving Children

United Hatzalah has documented several paediatric anaphylaxis cases demonstrating their response:

1. **Milk allergy case**: A child experienced anaphylaxis from accidental dairy exposure. United Hatzalah volunteers arrived and administered epinephrine, stabilising the child before MDA ambulance transport.

2. **Dairy doughnut case (December 2025)**: A child with a known dairy allergy accidentally consumed a dairy-filled doughnut during Hanukkah and went into anaphylactic shock. United Hatzalah medics responded rapidly and administered an EpiPen, followed by hospital transfer.

These cases illustrate the real-world pattern in Israel: food-triggered anaphylaxis in children, often from dairy or sesame, with United Hatzalah first responders providing initial epinephrine before MDA ambulance arrival.

## Treatment Protocol -- Children

### Immediate Actions
1. **Remove the offending agent** if possible (e.g., remove food from mouth, remove bee stinger)
2. **Call for emergency help** -- 101 (MDA) or 1221 (United Hatzalah)
3. **Secure the airway** -- assume airway swelling if facial swelling is present

### Patient Positioning (Children)
- **Conscious and breathing with difficulty**: Allow upright or position of comfort
- **Conscious without respiratory distress**: Supine with legs elevated
- **Unconscious and breathing**: Recovery position (on the side)
- **Unconscious and not breathing**: Begin paediatric CPR

### Epinephrine Administration
- Administer immediately -- do not delay
- Use appropriate dose for the child's weight (see table above)
- Pinch thigh for small/thin children
- Hold for 10 seconds
- Can be given through clothing

### Reassessment
- Check after 5 minutes
- Second dose if no improvement (with medical guidance if possible)
- Epinephrine effects last approximately 20 minutes

### Post-Administration
- All children must be transported to hospital after anaphylaxis, even if symptoms resolve
- Monitor for biphasic reaction

## Comparison with MDA Protocol (Children)

United Hatzalah's paediatric anaphylaxis protocol is **fully aligned** with MDA's guidelines:

- **Dosing**: Identical weight-based IM dosing schedule
- **Technique**: Same administration method with the added emphasis on pinching the thigh for small children
- **Repeat dosing**: Both allow up to 3 doses at 10-minute intervals (ALS level)
- **Diagnostic criteria**: Both use the Sampson classification
- **Positioning**: Both allow upright positioning for respiratory-predominant symptoms

**No material conflicts identified between MDA and United Hatzalah paediatric protocols.**

## Gaps and Limitations

- United Hatzalah does not publish a dedicated paediatric anaphylaxis protocol page separate from their general anaphylaxis content
- Specific guidance on children under 15 kg (no auto-injector available) is not addressed in their public-facing materials
- School/gan-specific guidance is not provided by United Hatzalah (this falls under MDA and Ministry of Education jurisdiction)
- No published guidance on common Israeli paediatric allergens or prevention strategies
