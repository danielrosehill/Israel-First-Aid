## Medically Significant Spiders in Israel

There are only **two spider species of medical significance** in the Mediterranean region (and therefore in Israel):

### 1. Mediterranean Recluse Spider (*Loxosceles rufescens*)
- **Hebrew**: עכביש הכינור הים-תיכוני
- **Also known as**: Violin spider, brown recluse (Mediterranean variety)
- **Habitat**: One of the most common spiders in citrus groves, buildings, and dark sheltered areas in Israel
- **Prevalence**: For every black widow bite, there are **hundreds** of recluse bites reported in Israel
- **Venom**: Contains sphingomyelinase D enzyme causing tissue necrosis

### 2. Mediterranean Black Widow (*Latrodectus tredecimguttatus*)
- **Hebrew**: אלמנה שחורה ים-תיכונית
- **Also known as**: European black widow
- **Habitat**: Dry, warm areas; under stones, in low vegetation, outbuildings
- **Prevalence**: Less common than recluse bites but potentially more dangerous systemically
- **Venom**: Neurotoxin (alpha-latrotoxin) affecting the nervous system

## First Aid Protocol -- Spider Bites (General)

### Immediate Actions

1. **Stay calm** -- most spider bites in Israel are not life-threatening
2. **Move away from the spider** -- do not attempt to catch it, but if safely possible, note its appearance or take a photo for identification
3. **Clean the wound** with mild soap and water
4. **Apply a cool compress** -- clean cloth dampened with cold water or wrapped ice pack, 15 minutes per hour, to reduce pain and swelling
5. **Keep the bitten area elevated** and as still as possible
6. **Do NOT apply a tourniquet** -- spider venom is not stopped by tourniquets
7. **Do NOT cut, suck, or apply suction** to the bite wound
8. **Do NOT apply heat** to the bite

### When to Call MDA (101) or Poison Center (04-7771900)

Call immediately if:
- Severe pain at the bite site or spreading pain
- Muscle cramps or spasms (especially abdominal -- suggests black widow)
- Difficulty breathing
- Nausea, vomiting, fever
- Spreading redness, swelling, or discolouration
- The victim is a child, elderly, or has pre-existing health conditions
- You suspect a black widow or recluse spider bite
- Any systemic symptoms (symptoms beyond the bite site)

## Mediterranean Recluse Spider Bite (*Loxosceles rufescens*)

### Recognition
- Bite is often **painless initially** -- victim may feel only a "pricking sensation"
- Within hours: burning sensation, oedema (swelling), painful blister
- Characteristic **blue-white-red concentric pattern** develops around the bite
- Progressive necrosis (tissue death) with dark eschar (scab) formation
- Healing is very slow: 2-5 weeks to months

### Systemic Symptoms (approximately 50% of cases)
- Fever, headache, myalgia (muscle pain)
- Malaise, fatigue
- Rarely: haemolysis (red blood cell destruction), kidney injury

### First Aid
1. Clean wound and apply cool compress
2. Elevate the bitten extremity
3. Call Poison Center (04-7771900) for guidance
4. Seek medical attention -- do not wait for symptoms to worsen
5. **Do NOT attempt to cut out or excise the bite wound** -- early surgical excision is contraindicated and worsens outcomes

### Medical Treatment (Hospital)
- Conservative local wound care (cleaning, appropriate dressings)
- Analgesics (pain relief) and tetanus prophylaxis
- Topical/oral antihistamines and corticosteroids for symptom management
- Antibiotics ONLY if secondary infection develops (culture-guided)
- Surgical debridement of eschar delayed until at least 5 weeks post-bite
- **No antivenom available** for Loxosceles bites outside South America

## Mediterranean Black Widow Bite (*Latrodectus tredecimguttatus*)

### Recognition
- Local pain at bite site (90% of cases, onset 15 minutes to 1 hour)
- Two small fang marks may be visible
- Pain may spread to entire limb, abdomen, or back

### Systemic Symptoms
- Muscle spasms and cramps (often severe)
- Abdominal pain (35% of cases) -- can mimic acute abdomen
- Back pain (45% of cases)
- Localised sweating (diaphoresis) in 55% of cases
- Nausea, vomiting, fever
- "Facies latrodectismica" -- facial grimacing with periorbital oedema
- Respiratory symptoms in severe cases

### First Aid
1. Clean wound and apply cool compress
2. Keep the victim calm and still
3. **Call MDA at 101 immediately** -- black widow bites require medical evaluation
4. Call Poison Center (04-7771900)
5. Monitor breathing -- respiratory compromise is possible in severe cases

### Medical Treatment (Hospital)
- Hospitalise symptomatic children, elderly, or those with cardiac conditions
- Analgesics and antihistamines
- **Antivenom** (1-2 vials IV over 1-2 hours) for severe cases
- Calcium gluconate is considered **ineffective** despite historical use
- Tetanus prophylaxis

## Diagnostic Warning

Spider bites are frequently misdiagnosed. Medical literature notes:

> "Spider bites have no pathognomonic signs or symptoms; therefore, most diagnoses are presumptive."

In one study, **83.5% of cases presenting as "spider bites" were actually skin/soft-tissue infections** from other causes (bacterial infections, allergic reactions, other arthropod bites, or dermatoses).

A verified diagnosis requires expert identification of the actual spider specimen collected at the time of the bite.
