## First Aid Protocol (from United Hatzalah materials)

### What TO Do

1. **Calm the victim** -- reassure the child, reduce anxiety. Stress and agitation increase heart rate and accelerate venom distribution.
2. **Immobilise the stung limb** -- keep the affected limb still to slow venom spread.
3. **Cool the sting area** -- apply an ice pack or cold compress to the sting site. United Hatzalah specifically states that cooling "relieves pain and slows the spread of venom."
4. **Evacuate to hospital rapidly** -- transport the child to a hospital as quickly as possible for monitoring and treatment.
5. **Monitor for delayed reactions** -- United Hatzalah warns that "some scorpions can cause delayed reactions," making hospital observation essential.

### What to Watch For

United Hatzalah's article describes the following systemic effects of scorpion venom:

**Nervous system effects:**
- Restlessness
- Altered consciousness
- Seizures
- Numbness
- Breathing difficulties

**Cardiovascular effects:**
- Heart failure (in severe cases)
- Pulmonary oedema
- Dangerous heart rhythms (arrhythmias)
- Elevated dopamine and adrenaline levels

### Hospital Treatment

At hospital, the child may receive:
- Pain relief medication
- Supportive treatment for the cardiovascular and respiratory systems
- Monitoring for arrhythmias
- Scorpion antivenom (in rare, severe cases)

## Key Facts from United Hatzalah Article

- **21 scorpion species** exist in Israel; only 5 are medically significant.
- **Black scorpions are dangerous** -- the common belief that black scorpions are harmless is explicitly called out as incorrect. United Hatzalah states they can be "potentially lethal to adults" (and by extension, even more dangerous for children).
- Scorpion venom is **neurotoxic** and **cardiotoxic**.

## Prevention Advice (from United Hatzalah)

- Remove piles of waste, rubble, and wood from the vicinity of living areas and playgrounds.
- Avoid inserting hands into holes or cracks.
- Use tools rather than bare hands when moving objects outdoors.
- Shake out sleeping bags and shoes before use.

## Gaps and Limitations

- **No child-specific protocol** was found from United Hatzalah. The general scorpion sting article applies to all ages.
- **No English-language version** of the scorpion sting article was found on israelrescue.org.
- The article on 1221.org.il does not specify observation times, antivenom indications, or dosing -- these are hospital-level decisions beyond first aid scope.
- The article does not mention the Israel Poison Information Center phone number.
