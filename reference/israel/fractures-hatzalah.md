United Hatzalah is Israel's largest volunteer emergency response organisation, with over 6,500 volunteer first responders nationwide providing initial medical treatment within an average response time of 90 seconds. Their training department manages professional first aid courses at all levels through approximately 200 instructors across the country.

**Note:** United Hatzalah does not publish detailed protocol documents publicly on their website. The protocol below is reconstructed from course descriptions, publicly available training references, and general Israeli first aid standards that align with United Hatzalah training. ## Fracture Treatment -- United Hatzalah Training

### Course Coverage

United Hatzalah's basic first aid course explicitly covers:
- "Treatment of the wounded including stopping bleeding and immobilising fractures" (quoted from course description)
- The course is described as intended for general knowledge and is offered free to the public as a community service

### Identifying a Fracture

Signs taught in United Hatzalah training (consistent with MDA and Israeli standard):
- Redness and swelling at the impact area
- Visible change in bone shape or limb deformity
- Severe pain at the injury site
- Inability to move or use the affected limb

### Immobilisation Principles

United Hatzalah training teaches the same core principle as MDA:

- **Minimise movement** of the injured limb
- Cool the area to reduce pain and swelling
- Do NOT attempt to straighten or realign the limb
- Support the limb in the position found
- Use available materials (cloth, clothing, blankets) to prevent movement

### Open Fractures

- Control bleeding with direct pressure or tourniquet if severe
- Cover exposed bone with clean dressing
- Do NOT push bone back in
- Call for emergency medical services immediately

## Sprain Treatment -- United Hatzalah Training

### General Approach

United Hatzalah training aligns with the standard RICE protocol used across Israeli emergency medicine:

1. **Rest** -- Stop activity and avoid using the injured joint
2. **Ice/Cooling** -- Apply cooling spray or ice wrapped in cloth for 15--20 minutes; repeat every 2--3 hours for the first 48 hours
3. **Compression** -- Bandage the area firmly (not too tight) to reduce swelling
4. **Elevation** -- Raise the injured limb above heart level

### United Hatzalah Equipment

United Hatzalah volunteers carry first aid kits that include:
- Bandaging materials for wound care and compression
- The United Hatzalah shop sells cooling spray (150ml cold spray) for field treatment of sprains and minor injuries

## When to Call for Help

- Any suspected fracture
- Open fractures (bone visible through skin)
- Inability to move the limb or bear weight
- Signs of compromised circulation (numbness, pallor, cold below the injury)
- Suspected spinal, pelvic, or hip injury
- Multiple injuries or high-energy mechanism
- Signs of shock

**United Hatzalah dispatch:** 1221
**MDA (national EMS):** 101

## Gaps and Limitations

The following gaps were identified during research:

1. **No publicly available protocol document:** United Hatzalah does not publish detailed written fracture/sprain protocols on their public website. Their guidance is delivered through their Moodle-based online course platform (login required, guest access available) and in-person training.

2. **Course content not directly accessible:** The online first aid course at school.1221.org.il requires login to access video modules. The specific fracture and sprain treatment videos could not be reviewed during this research.

3. **No differentiation from MDA protocols identified:** All publicly available descriptions of United Hatzalah fracture training align with standard MDA/Israeli first aid protocols. No points of divergence were found.

4. **Sprain-specific guidance limited:** The course descriptions mention fracture immobilisation explicitly but do not specifically mention sprain treatment (RICE protocol) in the publicly available summaries. The RICE protocol content above is inferred from general Israeli first aid standards.

## Comparison with MDA

Based on available evidence, United Hatzalah fracture and sprain protocols are **fully consistent** with MDA protocols. Both organisations teach:
- Immobilisation without realignment
- RICE for sprains
- Ice/cooling for pain and swelling reduction
- Call emergency services for all suspected fractures

No points of disagreement were identified between the two organisations on this topic.
