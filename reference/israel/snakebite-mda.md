## Venomous Snakes in Israel

Three species of venomous snakes in Israel are considered medically dangerous to humans:

| Species | Hebrew Name | Common Name | Distribution | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| *Daboia (Vipera) palaestinae* | צפע ארצישראלי | Palestine viper | Central and northern Israel | Most common; 100-300 bites/year in Israel. Declared Israel's national snake. |
| *Echis coloratus* | אפעה מדברי | Painted saw-scaled viper | Throughout Israel, usually less-populated/desert areas | Severe systemic venom effects |
| *Atractaspis engaddensis* | שחרור | Israeli mole viper | Southern regions only | Rarest of all venomous snakes in Israel; cardiotoxic venom; **no antivenom available** |

**Note**: Eight species of venomous snakes are endemic to Israel, but only these three are considered dangerous to humans.

## First Aid Protocol -- What To Do

### 1. Call MDA immediately -- 101

Call Magen David Adom at **101**. Paramedics at the MDA dispatch centre will provide immediate telephone guidance until the medical team arrives. Remain calm when calling -- do not create panic, which adds stress to the victim.

### 2. Keep the victim calm and still

- Reassure the victim; reduce stress and anxiety
- Have the victim lie down
- **Immobility and a low heart rate are critical** to slowing the spread of venom through the body

### 3. Position the bitten limb

- Position the bitten limb **below heart level**
- The limb should **not move at all**
- Immobilise (splint) the affected limb against the body

### 4. Remove constrictive items

- Remove jewellery, watches, rings, and tight clothing from the bite area
- The bite area **will swell** (אזור ההכשה יכול להתנפח)

### 5. Clean the wound

- Wash the bite area with **soap and water only**
- Do **NOT** use alcohol-based disinfectants (לא להשתמש בחומר חיטוי שמבוסס על אלכוהול)

### 6. Identify the snake if possible

- If possible, **photograph the snake from at least 2 metres away** while remaining safe
- If the snake is dead, keep it to show medical staff
- Do **NOT** attempt to catch or kill the snake (snakes are a protected species in Israel under the Wildlife Protection Law)

### 7. Monitor for allergic/anaphylactic reaction

Watch for warning signs:
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Swelling beyond the bite area, rash, itching
- Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting
- Changes in consciousness
- Drop in blood pressure

## What NOT To Do

These are explicitly prohibited in MDA guidelines:

| Prohibited Action | Hebrew | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|--------|
| Do NOT apply a tourniquet | אין להניח חוסם עורקים | Traps venom locally, causes tissue damage |
| Do NOT suck the venom out | אין למצוץ את הארס | Ineffective and dangerous |
| Do NOT cut the bite area | אין לחתוך את אזור ההכשה | Increases infection risk, does not help |
| Do NOT cool/ice the area | אין לקרר את האזור | Can worsen tissue damage |
| Do NOT give food or drink | אין לתת לנפגע לשתות | May complicate treatment |
| Do NOT induce vomiting | -- | Not indicated |
| Do NOT give aspirin/ibuprofen/naproxen | -- | Can worsen bleeding (viper venom is often anticoagulant) |

## Factors Affecting Severity

Response time and severity vary based on:
- Victim's body weight
- Victim's cardiac/health condition
- Snake species and amount of venom injected
- Psychological reaction (panic increases heart rate and venom spread)
- Those with severe allergies may face life-threatening complications within minutes

## Hospital Treatment and Antivenom

### Antivenom

- **Product**: Daboia (Vipera) palaestinae / Echis coloratus polyvalent antiserum
- **Source**: Equine-derived (horse serum)
- **Manufacturer**: Kamada Ltd., Rehovot, Israel (manufactured at GMP standards since 2011)
- **Note**: There is **no antivenom available** for *Atractaspis engaddensis* (Israeli mole viper)
- **Dosage (from clinical literature)**: 50 mL dosing has been shown effective and safe for systemic and progressive local manifestations

### Antivenom administration criteria

Antivenom is administered according to hospital protocol to patients with:
- Systemic symptoms (nausea, vomiting, hypotension, tachycardia)
- Severe or progressive local reaction (expanding swelling, clinical deterioration)
- A second dose may be given if systemic deterioration or worsening local reaction occurs

## Common Clinical Presentation (for reference)

Most common local tissue reaction:
- Pain and oedema (swelling) at bite site

Most common systemic reactions:
- Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting
- Tachycardia (rapid heart rate)
- Hypotension (low blood pressure)

Mortality rate for *Daboia palaestinae* bites: **0.5% to 2%** (with antivenom treatment available since mid-20th century)

## Israel-Specific Context

- **Peak season**: Snakebites are more common in warm months (spring/summer) when snakes are active and people spend time outdoors
- **Climate**: Israel's desert and Mediterranean climate means snakes are encountered in hiking trails, agricultural areas, and even urban edges
- **Legal note**: All snakes in Israel are protected under the Wildlife Protection Law (חוק הגנת חיית הבר). Killing snakes is prohibited.
- **Emergency number**: MDA = **101**
