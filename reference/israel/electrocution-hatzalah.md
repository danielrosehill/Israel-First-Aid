## Source Authority

United Hatzalah (Ichud Hatzalah) is Israel's largest independent volunteer emergency medical service organization, with over 8,000 volunteer first responders nationwide. Volunteers are trained to MDA-equivalent standards and operate under Israeli Ministry of Health protocols. United Hatzalah operates ambulances and "ambucycles" and provides rapid first response alongside MDA.

**Note on source availability:** United Hatzalah's website (1221.org.il) does not publish a dedicated public-facing electrocution first aid protocol page. The electrical conduction page found on their site covers cardiac electrophysiology education for volunteers, not electrocution injury treatment. Their first aid training courses include electrocution response, but the detailed protocol is not published online. The protocol below is synthesised from United Hatzalah volunteer training principles (which align with Israeli Ministry of Health standards) and cross-referenced with the MDA protocol.

## Protocol (Based on United Hatzalah Volunteer Training Standards)

United Hatzalah volunteers are trained to the same Israeli Ministry of Health first aid and EMT standards as MDA personnel. The electrocution response protocol taught in United Hatzalah courses is consistent with the MDA protocol. Key elements:

### 1. Scene Safety -- Absolute Priority

- **DO NOT touch the victim** while they may still be in contact with the electrical source
- Assess the scene before approaching
- Be aware of "chain electrocution" risk -- multiple rescuers have been injured in sequence

### 2. Disconnect the Power Source

- **Low-voltage:** Switch off at the mains, unplug the device, or use a dry non-conducting object to separate the victim from the source
- **High-voltage:** DO NOT approach. Call MDA (101), Fire (102), and Israel Electric Corporation (103). Maintain a safe distance of at least 10 metres.
- Ensure any object used to separate the victim is completely dry

### 3. Call for Help

- Call **MDA at 101** and/or **United Hatzalah at 1221**
- For high-voltage incidents, also call **102** (Fire) and **103** (IEC)

### 4. Primary Assessment

- Check consciousness and breathing
- If unconscious and breathing: place in recovery position
- If unconscious and not breathing: begin CPR immediately

### 5. CPR

- Standard adult CPR: 30 compressions to 2 breaths
- Rate: 100--120 compressions per minute
- Depth: 5--6 cm
- Use AED if available -- dry the chest before applying pads
- Do NOT stop CPR until professional EMS arrives

### 6. Burns Assessment

- Electrical burns may have small entry/exit wounds concealing severe internal damage
- Cool visible burns with cool running water once scene is safe
- Cover loosely with clean dressing
- Do NOT apply creams or home remedies

### 7. Spinal Precautions

- If the victim fell or was thrown, suspect spinal injury
- Minimise movement; wait for professional extraction if possible

### 8. Hospital Transport

- **All electrocution victims require hospital evaluation** regardless of apparent injury severity
- Delayed cardiac arrhythmias, internal burns, and rhabdomyolysis are common complications

## Differences from MDA Protocol

No substantive differences have been identified. United Hatzalah volunteer training follows the same Israeli Ministry of Health protocols as MDA. Both organizations emphasise:

1. Scene safety and power disconnection before contact
2. Immediate CPR for unresponsive/non-breathing victims
3. Mandatory hospital evaluation for all electrocution cases
4. Calling 101 (MDA) for ambulance dispatch
