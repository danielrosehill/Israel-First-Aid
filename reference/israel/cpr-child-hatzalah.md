## Age Definitions

| Category | Age Range |
|----------|-----------|
| Newborn (יילוד) | Birth to 1 month |
| Infant (תינוק) | 1 month to 1 year |
| Child (ילד) | 1 year until onset of puberty (approx. ages 10-14) |

Puberty is defined by the appearance of secondary sexual characteristics.

## Initial Assessment (within 30 seconds)

1. **Ensure scene safety.**
2. **Check responsiveness:**
   - **Infants**: Stimulate the soles of the feet.
   - **Children**: Gently squeeze the trapezius muscles; call the child's name or use familiar sounds.
3. **Check breathing and pulse** (no more than 10 seconds).
   - If no breathing and no pulse (or pulse < 60 bpm with signs of poor perfusion): begin CPR.

## CPR Sequence: C-A-B (Compressions - Airway - Breathing)

### C - Chest Compressions

**Rate**: 100-120 compressions per minute (all ages).

**Depth**: Approximately one-third to one-half the anterior-posterior chest diameter.

**Hand placement**: Lower third of the sternum. Avoid the xiphoid process.

| Patient | Technique |
|---------|-----------|
| Infant (single rescuer) | Two-finger technique: 2 fingers on lower third of sternum |
| Infant (two rescuers) | Two-thumb encircling hands technique: encircle torso, compress with both thumbs, other fingers support the back |
| Child | Heel of one hand OR two hands, depending on child's size |

**Key points**:
- Allow full chest recoil after each compression.
- Minimise interruptions in compressions.

### A - Airway

- **Children**: Mild head-tilt chin-lift (slight extension).
- **Infants**: Neutral/sniffing position (do NOT hyperextend the neck).

### B - Breathing (Rescue Breaths)

- Each breath delivered over **1 second**.
- Deliver until visible chest rise -- no more.
- Avoid excessive volume or pressure (risk of gastric distension).
- Use appropriately sized bag-valve-mask if available:
  - Infant-sized BVM for infants.
  - Adult-sized BVM for children (with appropriate mask size).

## Compression-to-Breath Ratios

| Scenario | Ratio |
|----------|-------|
| Single rescuer (infant or child) | 30:2 |
| Two rescuers (infant or child) | 15:2 |
| With advanced airway in place | Continuous compressions at 100-120/min; 1 breath every 6-8 seconds (8-10 breaths/min) |

## AED / Defibrillator

- Attach defibrillator as soon as available, especially in suspected sudden cardiac arrest.
- Use **pediatric pads/attenuator** if available.
- If the child's chest is too small for standard pad placement (both pads on anterior chest), place one pad on the **anterior chest** and one on the **back** (anteroposterior placement).

## When to Call for Help

- If **alone**: Perform 2 minutes of CPR (approximately 5 cycles of 30:2) FIRST, then call 101 (MDA) or send someone.
- If **another person is present**: One person calls 101 immediately while the other begins CPR.

**Rationale**: Paediatric cardiac arrest is most often caused by respiratory failure or hypoxia (unlike adults where primary cardiac events predominate). Early ventilation and compressions are critical.

## Key Differences from Adult CPR

- **Cause**: Children typically arrest from respiratory failure leading to hypoxia, not primary cardiac events.
- **Rescue breaths are essential**: Unlike adult CPR where compression-only CPR may be acceptable, paediatric CPR should include ventilations.
- **Compression technique**: Adapted for smaller body size (fingers/one hand vs. two hands).
- **Call for help timing**: Lone rescuer performs 2 minutes of CPR before calling (adults: call immediately).
