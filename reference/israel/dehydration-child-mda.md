## General MDA Dehydration Guidance

### Definition
Dehydration occurs when the body loses more fluids than it takes in. Fluids are lost through breathing, sweating, urination, vomiting, and diarrhoea.

### Israeli Climate Context
- Israel's desert and semi-arid climate creates heightened risk during summer months (May--October)
- Sharav (hamsin) heat waves are particularly dangerous
- MDA regularly issues public warnings about dehydration during heat waves
- Children on school trips and outdoor activities are especially vulnerable

### First Aid Steps (General -- all ages)
1. Move patient to shade/cool environment
2. Encourage oral fluid intake (preferred over IV for mild/moderate cases)
3. Provide electrolyte-containing fluids (not plain water alone)
4. Avoid cola, fruit juice, plain water, or tea (do not adequately replace electrolytes)
5. If patient cannot drink (vomiting, swallowing difficulty), IV fluids may be needed
6. Monitor vital signs
7. Transport to hospital if severe

## Child-Specific Considerations (from Israeli emergency medicine context)

### Why Children Dehydrate Faster
- Higher metabolic rate
- Higher surface-area-to-body-mass ratio
- Greater proportion of body water (infants ~75% vs adults ~60%)
- Less ability to communicate thirst
- Immature kidneys less efficient at conserving water
- Common childhood illnesses (gastroenteritis) cause rapid fluid loss

### Signs of Dehydration in Children
**Mild**: Dry mouth, decreased urine output, slightly sunken eyes, thirst
**Moderate**: No tears when crying, sunken fontanelle (in infants under 18 months), decreased skin turgor, lethargy
**Severe**: Very sunken fontanelle, very dry mucous membranes, rapid weak pulse, cool/mottled extremities, altered consciousness

### Fontanelle Check (Infants)
- Sunken anterior fontanelle is a key indicator of dehydration in infants
- Open until approximately 18 months of age
- Assessed by gentle palpation

### ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution) for Children
- ORS packets available at Israeli pharmacies
- Mix as directed on packet
- For infants: small frequent sips with spoon or syringe
- Do NOT use homemade salt/sugar solutions unless ORS unavailable
- Continue breastfeeding if applicable

### When to Call 101 (MDA)
- Child refuses to drink
- Persistent vomiting
- Blood in stool
- Fever above 40 degrees C
- Signs of severe dehydration (lethargy, sunken fontanelle, no tears)
- Infant under 3 months with any signs of dehydration
