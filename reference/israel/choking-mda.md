## Definitions

**Choking (חנק מגוף זר / השתנקות)**: Blockage of the airway by a foreign body (food, object, liquid), preventing normal breathing.

- **Partial obstruction (חסימה חלקית)**: The person can still cough, speak, or breathe, though with difficulty. The airway is not fully blocked.
- **Complete obstruction (חסימה מלאה)**: The person cannot speak, cough effectively, or breathe. This is a medical emergency.

**Critical time window**: Without adequate oxygen supply, irreversible brain damage can occur within **4 minutes** (some sources cite 3-6 minutes).

---

## Recognition / Signs of Choking

- The person grasps their neck with one or both hands (universal choking sign)
- Inability to speak, cough, or breathe
- Face turning red, then blue (cyanosis)
- The person may stand up suddenly from their chair
- High-pitched or wheezing sounds when trying to breathe
- Loss of consciousness (late sign)

## Protocol: Conscious Adult with Complete Obstruction

### Step 1 -- Assess the situation

Ask the person: **"Are you choking? Can you speak?"**

- If the person **can cough forcefully and can speak**: **DO NOT intervene.** Allow them to attempt to expel the foreign object on their own. Coughing is the body's natural defence mechanism and is often effective.
- If the person **cannot speak, cough effectively, or breathe**: Proceed immediately with the Heimlich maneuver.

### Step 2 -- Call for help

- Instruct a bystander to call **101 (MDA)** immediately
- If alone, begin life-saving actions first, then call 101 as soon as possible

### Step 3 -- Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver / תמרון היימליך)

1. Stand **behind** the choking person
2. Wrap your arms around their **waist**
3. Make a **fist** with one hand
4. Place the fist **above the navel (belly button), below the breastbone (sternum)**, with the thumb side against the abdomen
5. Grasp the fist with your other hand
6. Perform **rapid, forceful thrusts inward and upward** -- as if trying to lift the person
7. **Repeat** until the foreign object is expelled

### Step 4 -- If the person loses consciousness

1. **Stop** abdominal thrusts
2. **Lower** the person carefully to the ground onto a firm, flat surface
3. **Call 101 (MDA)** if not already done
4. **Begin CPR** (cardiopulmonary resuscitation):
   - 30 chest compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths
   - Before each set of rescue breaths, look in the mouth for a visible object
   - If an object is visible, remove it carefully (finger sweep)
   - Continue CPR cycles until emergency services arrive or the person recovers

## Special Populations

| Population | Modification |
|---|---|
| **Pregnant women** | Use **chest thrusts** instead of abdominal thrusts (hands placed on the centre of the breastbone) |
| **Very obese individuals** | Use **chest thrusts** instead of abdominal thrusts |
| **Person in wheelchair** | Perform chest compressions while the person remains seated |
| **Self-treatment (alone)** | Lean over a firm object (chair back, countertop) and press the upper abdomen forcefully against it |

---

## Important Warnings

- **DO NOT perform abdominal thrusts** on a person who can cough forcefully and speak -- you may worsen the situation
- **DO NOT perform back blows (slaps on the back)**: According to the Israeli sources reviewed, the traditional practice of hitting a choking person on the back is described as **incorrect and potentially harmful** ("incorrect and can cause harm" / "will only interfere"). This is a notable difference from some international protocols (e.g., ERC, British Red Cross) that recommend alternating 5 back blows with 5 abdominal thrusts.
- **DO NOT perform blind finger sweeps** -- only remove an object from the mouth if you can see it
- **DO NOT attempt to give water** to a choking person

---

## Important Note on Back Blows

There is a significant divergence between the MDA-aligned Israeli protocol and international guidelines on back blows:

- **MDA / Israeli protocol**: Abdominal thrusts (Heimlich) only for conscious adults. Back blows are explicitly discouraged for adults in multiple Israeli sources reviewed.
- **AHA (USA, 2025)**: Recommends alternating 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts.
- **ERC (Europe, 2025)**: Recommends 5 back blows first, then 5 abdominal thrusts if back blows fail.

This divergence should be verified against MDA's most current training materials, as guidelines evolve. The most recent MDA training booklets (2024/2026) could not be fully accessed during this research.

## Causes of Choking (for context)

- **Eating too quickly** or talking/laughing while eating
- **Inadequately chewed food** (especially meat, bread)
- **Alcohol consumption** (reduces the gag reflex)
- **Dentures** (reduce sensation in the mouth)
- **Tongue collapse** (in unconscious patients, the tongue can fall backward and block the airway)
- **Aspiration** of secretions, saliva, or stomach acid
