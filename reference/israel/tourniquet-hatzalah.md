## United Hatzalah's Position on Tourniquets

United Hatzalah strongly favours tourniquet use for limb hemorrhage. Their training materials state:

> "Indirect pressure -- tourniquet application -- is preferable because it is powerful, rapid, and more likely to stop bleeding with certainty."

This is notably more pro-tourniquet than many international guidelines, reflecting Israel's security context and the frequency of penetrating trauma from terror attacks and conflict.

---

## When to Apply a Tourniquet

Per United Hatzalah protocols, apply a tourniquet when:

1. **Limb hemorrhage is life-threatening** and direct pressure is insufficient
2. **Amputation** (partial or complete)
3. **Severe crushing injury**
4. **Multi-casualty situations** where sustained direct pressure on each wound is not feasible
5. **Active threat environment** where you need to move the casualty quickly

### Critical Rule
**"Do not initiate resuscitation before controlling severe bleeding."** -- Hemorrhage control (tourniquet application) takes priority over CPR in trauma patients.

## United Hatzalah Hemorrhage Control Sequence

From their Hebrew-language hemorrhage control guide:

### Types of Hemorrhage
- **Arterial bleeding**: Pulsing, forceful spurts synchronised with heartbeat -- most dangerous
- **Venous bleeding**: Steady flow
- **Capillary bleeding**: Slow flow -- least dangerous

### Shock Recognition
- Rapid pulse is the initial shock indicator
- "Do not assume tachycardia stems from stress or pain until proven otherwise"
- Early recognition of developing shock is critical

### Control Methods (Progressive)

1. **Direct Pressure**: Apply hand or sterile gauze directly to the bleeding site; maintain continuous pressure to allow clot formation

2. **Israeli Bandage (Emergency Bandage)**: Dual-pad design creates direct pressure while wrapping around wound; verify pulse distal to bandage afterward

3. **Wound Packing**: For large wounds in limbs, shoulders, groin -- fill wound with special gauze to create internal pressure, then secure with bandaging

4. **Pressure Points**: Strategic body locations where arteries pass near skin over bone (neck, upper arm, thigh, wrist) -- compress to reduce downstream blood flow

5. **Arterial Tourniquet**: Applied when direct pressure fails or circumstances prevent it; stops all circulation distal to application point

## United Hatzalah Trauma Kit

All 7,000+ United Hatzalah volunteers carry portable trauma kits containing:
- **CAT tourniquets** (Combat Application Tourniquet)
- **Israeli bandages / emergency bandages**
- **Leatherman Raptor scissors** (for rapid clothing removal to access wounds)
- **High-quality flashlights**
- Additional bleeding control supplies

The CAT tourniquet is available for purchase through the Hatzalah Shop (shop.1221.org.il) for civilian use.

## Tourniquet Conversion

United Hatzalah protocols note that tourniquets "may be replaced with direct pressure under medical direction once circumstances change." This is a professional-level decision, not for civilian bystanders.

## Differences from MDA Protocol

| Aspect | MDA | United Hatzalah |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Tourniquet priority | Part of progressive sequence (try direct pressure first) | "Preferable" for limb hemorrhage -- more aggressive early use |
| Overall philosophy | "Save and run" / "Scoop and run" | Hemorrhage control before resuscitation |
| Practical difference | Minimal -- both prioritise hemorrhage control in trauma | Both follow same Ministry of Health standards |

**Note**: Both organisations train under Ministry of Health oversight and follow fundamentally compatible protocols. The difference is primarily one of emphasis in public-facing training materials. MDA's written guidelines present a more graduated approach; United Hatzalah's training materials emphasise tourniquet application more aggressively as a first-line intervention.
