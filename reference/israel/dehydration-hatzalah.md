## Protocol Summary (Translated from Hebrew)

Hatzalah Without Borders publishes a complete guide titled "What to Do in Case of Dehydration?" (Mah Osim b'Mikre shel Hit'yabshut?) as part of their life-saving guides series.

### Definition
"When the body loses more fluids than it consumes, dehydration occurs."

### Symptoms
- Increased thirst
- Dry mouth
- Fatigue and general weakness
- Dizziness
- Dry mouth and skin
- Reduced urination or absence of urge to urinate

### First Aid — Key Principle: Gradual Rehydration

**Critical warning from Hatzalah Without Borders:**
> "Rapid drinking and rapid fluid replacement during dehydration can worsen the symptoms the patient is suffering from, due to disruption of the balance between blood salts [electrolytes]."

### Treatment Steps
1. Replace lost fluid volume gradually
2. Give small sips of fluid — use a straw if helpful
3. Be gentle and measured in fluid replacement
4. Do NOT give large quantities of water quickly
5. If symptoms do not improve, seek medical attention

### Hospital Treatment
When dehydration is severe, hospital treatment includes:
- Intravenous fluid infusion to restore fluid volume
- Blood tests to diagnose the underlying causes of dehydration

---

## Comparison with MDA

Hatzalah Without Borders' guidance is consistent with MDA protocols on the key points:
- Both emphasise **gradual rehydration** — small sips, not rapid drinking
- Both warn that rapid fluid replacement can cause electrolyte imbalance
- Both recommend seeking professional medical help for severe cases

MDA provides additional detail on:
- Distinguishing dehydration from heat stroke
- Active cooling measures
- Specific criteria for calling 101
- ORS preparation instructions
