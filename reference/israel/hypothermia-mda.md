## Definition

Hypothermia occurs when body temperature drops below 35 degrees C (95 degrees F). It can develop rapidly (e.g., cold water immersion) or gradually through prolonged exposure to cold environments.

## Recognition — Warning Signs

### Mild Hypothermia (32-35 degrees C / 90-95 degrees F)
- Shivering
- Fatigue, hunger, nausea
- Weak pulse, slow movements
- Pale, cold skin (even under clothing)
- Increased urination
- Confusion and impaired judgment
- Decreased functioning

### Moderate Hypothermia (28-32 degrees C / 82-90 degrees F)
- Apathy regarding own condition
- Slurred speech
- Severe decrease in heart rate
- Muscle rigidity
- Shivering STOPS (dangerous sign)
- Bluish skin colour
- Low blood pressure
- Significantly impaired cognition
- Dilated pupils
- Cardiac arrhythmias
- Possible loss of consciousness

### Severe Hypothermia (below 28 degrees C / 82 degrees F)
- Pulmonary oedema
- Dangerously low blood pressure
- Minimal urine output
- Absent reflexes
- Coma
- Cardiac arrest
- Brain death risk

## First Aid Steps (MDA-Referenced Protocol)

1. **Call MDA immediately** — dial 101
2. **Move to warmth** — bring the person to a warm, dry shelter; protect from wind and rain
3. **Remove wet clothing** promptly and replace with dry layers
4. **Dress in lightweight layered clothing** that allows some movement (movement generates heat)
5. **Cover with blankets** — wrap in blankets and layers; cover the head
6. **Warm the room** to approximately 22 degrees C (72 degrees F) if indoors
7. **Provide warm drinks** — warm (NOT hot) beverages if the person is conscious; avoid alcohol and caffeine
8. **Apply heat packs** to the chest, abdomen, and back ONLY — never directly on bare skin (use a cloth barrier)
9. **Monitor breathing** — begin CPR if breathing stops
10. **Gradual rewarming** — warm the torso FIRST, not hands and feet

## Critical DO NOTs

- **Do NOT rub extremities** — friction can cause tissue damage and cardiac complications
- **Do NOT use direct intense heat** (hot water baths, heating pads directly on skin) — rapid rewarming can cause dangerous cardiac arrhythmias
- **Do NOT give alcohol** — it dilates blood vessels and accelerates heat loss
- **Do NOT give caffeine** — worsens hypothermia
- **Do NOT warm extremities first** — this can cause cold blood to rush to the heart, potentially triggering cardiac arrest ("afterdrop")
- **Do NOT apply vigorous external rewarming**

## Carbon Monoxide Warning (MDA-Specific)

MDA training materials specifically warn about CO poisoning risk when people use gas heaters or charcoal-burning fireplaces to combat cold:
- CO is colourless and odourless
- Symptoms: dizziness, nausea, vomiting, fatigue, weakness, loss of consciousness
- Action: shut off heating, ventilate the space, call 101

## Israeli Context

- **Desert nights**: The Negev desert can experience temperatures below freezing at night, catching hikers unprepared
- **Golan Heights and Mount Hermon**: Snow and sub-zero temperatures in winter; hikers and skiers at risk
- **Military context**: IDF soldiers on winter exercises in northern Israel
- **Homeless populations**: Urban hypothermia during cold spells in Jerusalem, Safed, and other elevated areas
- **Key risk periods**: December through February; sudden cold waves ("gal kor")
