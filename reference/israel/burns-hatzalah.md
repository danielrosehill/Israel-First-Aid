## Source Authority

United Hatzalah (Ichud Hatzalah) is Israel's largest volunteer emergency medical organisation with approximately 8,000 volunteers including paramedics, medics, and doctors. While not the official national EMS (that is MDA), United Hatzalah is a recognised and authoritative source of first aid training in Israel. Their basic first aid course covers adult and infant resuscitation, wound treatment, bleeding control, and fracture immobilisation.

## Burn Classification (from United Hatzalah)

### First-Degree Burns (Superficial)
- Damage is to the epidermis only
- Manifested by swelling of the skin and local redness
- Duration: 48--72 hours of symptoms
- Healing time: 5--10 days with complete healing

### Second-Degree Burns (Partial Thickness)
Two subtypes:
- **Shallow second-degree:** Damages the entire epidermis layer. Characterised by severe pain, redness, and blisters. Heals in 10--14 days with complete recovery if no infection occurs.
- **Deep second-degree:** Takes 25--35 days to heal. Can easily progress to third-degree if infected.

### Third-Degree Burns (Full Thickness)
- Tissue is brown-white or black in colour, and without sensation
- When pressure is applied to the burn's crust, there is no whitening or redness of the tissue because blood vessels are destroyed or blocked
- Requires professional medical treatment

## First Aid Protocol (from United Hatzalah)

### Cooling
- Rapid cooling prevents advancing heat damage, revives damaged cells, and relieves pain
- Use cold water on the burn area -- this is effective and safe for rapid removal of heat effects from the affected area
- United Hatzalah emphasises the importance of cooling as the primary first aid intervention

### Remove Restrictive Items
- Remove rings and jewellery from the burn area before swelling occurs

### Handle Clothing Carefully
- Remove clothing from the burn area
- If clothing is stuck to the burn, cut around it rather than pulling it off

### Position the Patient
- Transfer the patient to a lying-down position to reduce shock damage

## Burn Severity Thresholds (from United Hatzalah)

### Severe Burns (require emergency transport)
- Second-degree burns covering more than 25% body surface area in adults (more than 20% in children)
- Third-degree burns covering more than 10% body surface area
- Burns to hands, face, eyes, ears, or genitals
- Inhalation injuries
- Chemical or electrical burns

### Complications to Monitor
- Infection
- Respiratory issues
- Kidney dysfunction
- Hypovolemic shock within 24--48 hours post-injury

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

United Hatzalah's burns protocol is broadly consistent with MDA's guidelines. Key points of alignment:

- Both classify burns into three degrees with similar descriptions
- Both emphasise cooling as the primary first aid intervention
- Both recommend removing jewellery and cutting around stuck clothing
- Both use the same severity thresholds for emergency transport
- Both recommend positioning the patient lying down

**Minor differences noted:**
- United Hatzalah describes using "cold water" whereas MDA-aligned sources specify "cool (not cold) running water" -- this may be a translation nuance rather than a substantive difference
- United Hatzalah's published page does not specify a minimum cooling duration (MDA specifies at least 10 minutes)
- United Hatzalah's published page does not include specific guidance on covering the burn (MDA recommends cling film or clean plastic bag)

**Recommendation:** Where the two sources diverge, follow MDA guidance as the authoritative default per the project's source hierarchy.
