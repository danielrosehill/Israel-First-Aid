## General MDA Asthma Guidance

Based on MDA training materials and professional standards:

### Definition
Asthma is a chronic, obstructive, episodic respiratory disease characterised by inflammatory reactions in the lungs upon exposure to triggering factors, marked by airway hypersensitivity resulting in attacks of bronchial swelling, increased mucus secretion, and airway narrowing.

### First Aid Steps (BLS level -- all ages)
1. Help patient sit upright to maximise lung expansion
2. Calm and reassure the patient
3. Assist with prescribed rescue inhaler (Ventolin/salbutamol)
4. Administer high-concentration oxygen if available (10 L/min)
5. Obtain medical history (known asthma? medications? previous hospitalisations?)
6. Monitor for worsening symptoms
7. Call 101 if symptoms do not improve
8. Transport to medical facility

### Child-Specific Considerations (from MDA training context)
- Asthma is the most common chronic disease in children
- In Israel, estimated prevalence of 5--10% among children
- Children in gan (kindergarten) and school settings may have asthma action plans
- For children under 6: use inhaler with spacer device (Volumatic or similar)
- MDA course descriptions reference infant/child respiratory emergencies as part of their curriculum
- Children may not articulate breathing difficulty -- look for accessory muscle use, nasal flaring, intercostal retractions
- Approach calmly; speak to child directly, not only to parents
