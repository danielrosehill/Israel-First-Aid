## General MDA Eye Injury Guidance

Based on MDA training materials and course descriptions, the following principles apply:

### Types of Eye Injuries
- Chemical burns (acid or alkali)
- Foreign objects (dust, metal fragments, wood)
- Thermal burns
- Blunt trauma
- Penetrating injuries

### First Aid Steps (General -- all ages)
1. **Chemical burns**: Irrigate immediately with clean water for 15--30 minutes; tilt head so water runs away from unaffected eye
2. **Foreign objects**: Do not rub the eye; attempt gentle flushing with clean water; if object is embedded, do NOT attempt removal
3. **Penetrating injuries**: Do not apply pressure or bandage; use rigid shield over eye; transport supine to hospital
4. **Blunt trauma**: Apply cold compress gently; monitor for vision changes; seek medical evaluation

### Child-Specific Considerations (from general MDA pediatric guidance)
- Children are commonly exposed to eye injuries from playground equipment, toys, and household chemicals
- Children may not report vision changes accurately -- observe behaviour
- Approach calmly; explain procedures honestly to the child
- Allow child to sit with parent during assessment when possible
- In Israel, common household chemicals (bleach, cleaning products) are frequent causes of pediatric eye chemical burns
