## Protocol Summary (Translated from Hebrew)

Hatzalah Without Borders publishes a first aid guide for eye injuries as part of their "Life-Saving Guides" (Madrikhim l'Hatzalat Khayyim) series.

### Immediate Response

1. **Foreign object penetration or chemical exposure**: Rush to the nearest tap and rinse thoroughly with plenty of water
2. **Assessment**: After rinsing, evaluate whether additional medical assistance is needed

### Key Principles

- **Foreign objects**: Any penetrating injury from a foreign body requires immediate attention
- **Chemical exposure**: Any chemical splash toward the eyes demands urgent action — begin flushing immediately
- **Do not delay**: Professional medical evaluation should follow initial first aid

### When to Seek Emergency Care

- Severe pain persists after rinsing
- Vision changes occur
- Chemical exposure involves caustic substances (acids, alkalis)
- Object is embedded in the eye

---

## Comparison with MDA

Hatzalah Without Borders' guidance is brief and consistent with MDA protocols. Key alignment:
- Both emphasise immediate flushing with water for foreign bodies and chemical splashes
- Both recommend professional medical evaluation after initial treatment
- Neither organisation recommends attempting to remove embedded objects

MDA provides more detailed guidance on specific injury types (blunt trauma, lacerations, embedded objects) and specifies flushing duration (15-30 minutes for chemicals). The Hatzalah guide is more of a quick-reference summary.

## Note on United Hatzalah (Ichud Hatzalah / 1221.org.il)

**No specific eye injury protocol was found on United Hatzalah's website (1221.org.il).** Their publicly available content focuses on CPR, bleeding control, and fracture management. The Hatzalah Without Borders guide above is from a separate organisation (hatzalah.org.il), not Ichud Hatzalah (1221.org.il). Both are Israeli volunteer medical organisations, but they are distinct entities.
