## Spider Bite Protocol (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### Venomous Spider Species in Israel

**Brown Recluse Spider (ששן חום / Loxosceles rufescens)**
- Small, brown, long-legged spider
- Lives primarily indoors
- Bite usually barely felt; effects appear hours later
- Causes local tissue damage; rarely systemic illness involving clotting disorders
- Necrotic wound development possible at bite centre

**Black Widow Spider (אלמנה שחורה / Latrodectus tredecimguttatus)**
- Female's bite is the dangerous one
- Symptoms develop within minutes to hours
- **Local**: Pain and burning at bite site, spreading with redness along lymph pathways
- **Systemic**: Malaise, severe abdominal pain, dyspnoea, elevated blood pressure, excessive sweating
- Recovery typically requires 3--4 days; weakness persists for weeks
- **Antivenom is now available** in Israeli hospitals

### Important Note
Only a limited number of spider species can physically inject venom into human skin tissue.

## General Treatment (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### Field Management
- Follow ABC protocols with supportive care
- Treat as per general envenomation guidelines

### Critical Contraindications (explicitly stated by Ichud Hatzalah)
**"Do NOT apply tourniquets, do not bandage, do not cut, do not suck, do not cool"** -- this applies to all venomous animal injuries including spider bites.

### General Envenomation Principles (from Venomous Animals page)
- Venomous creatures most active during spring and summer, especially after winter hibernation when "gorged with venom"
- Local signs: wound, redness, pain, blisters, skin peeling, swelling, local heat
- Systemic effects vary by venom type:
  - Blood system toxins: interfere with clotting
  - Kidney/heart toxins: arrhythmias, cardiac arrest
  - Nerve/muscle toxins: act more slowly
- **First 6--8 hours are critical** for determining recovery and preventing permanent tissue damage
- All envenomations require urgent hospitalisation

### Scorpion Stings (related -- from same article)
- Approximately 21 scorpion species in Israel, five seriously dangerous
- Most dangerous: Yellow scorpion (deathstalker), thick-tailed black scorpion
- Venom affects nervous system and cardiovascular system
- Neurological effects: restlessness, altered consciousness, convulsions, numbness
- Cardiovascular: heart failure, arrhythmias, shock
- Field management: cool sting site with ice water; hospital monitoring essential

## Child-Specific Considerations

### From Ichud Hatzalah Materials
**GAP IDENTIFIED**: The venomous animal injury pages on 1221.org.il contain "no specific information regarding children or age-adjusted treatment protocols."

### Paediatric Vulnerability (from general medical knowledge applicable to Israeli context)
- **Greater venom effect per body weight**: same venom dose is proportionally more dangerous for a smaller child
- Faster onset of systemic symptoms
- Children may not report the bite (often painless initially)
- Infants and toddlers cannot describe symptoms -- look for irritability, unusual crying, poor feeding
- Black widow venom effects can be more severe in children, particularly respiratory symptoms
- Scorpion stings in children can cause more severe neurological effects

### Prevention in Israeli Context
- Clear overgrown vegetation near trails and homes
- Check sleeping bags before use (camping in Negev/desert)
- Wear high shoes and long clothing in affected areas
- Shake out shoes and clothing stored in wardrobes
- Brown recluse commonly found in undisturbed indoor spaces
- Black widow found in gardens, stone walls, sheds
