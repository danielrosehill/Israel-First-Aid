### Notes on source access

United Hatzalah does not publish a standalone public-facing choking protocol document on their website in the same way MDA does. Their choking guidance is delivered through:

1. **Free basic first aid course** available at `school.1221.org.il` (Moodle-based online platform)

3. **Family Safety training programme** -- covers treating choking victims
4. **Hatzalah Without Borders** (hatzalah.org.il) -- an affiliated organisation that published a detailed Heimlich guide

The protocol details below are drawn from the Hatzalah Without Borders article and United Hatzalah course descriptions. Where detailed protocol steps were not available from United Hatzalah directly, this is noted.

## Protocol Summary

Based on available United Hatzalah / Hatzalah Without Borders sources:

### Recognition

Identify that a person is choking and in distress -- struggling to breathe, unable to speak, grasping at throat.

### Immediate Actions

1. **Call emergency services immediately** while beginning rescue efforts -- do not wait
   - United Hatzalah dispatch: **1221**
   - MDA: **101**
2. **Begin life-saving actions** to dislodge the foreign object

### Treatment: Heimlich Maneuver (תמרון היימליך)

The Heimlich maneuver is the primary technique taught in United Hatzalah training for conscious adult choking victims:

1. Stand behind the choking person
2. Wrap arms around their waist
3. Place fist above the navel, thumb toward the abdomen
4. Grasp fist with other hand
5. Thrust forcefully upward and inward
6. Repeat until the object is expelled

### If the Person Loses Consciousness

- Lower to the ground
- Begin CPR
- Call emergency services if not already done

## Differences from MDA Protocol

No significant differences were identified between United Hatzalah's approach and MDA's protocol for adult choking. Both organisations teach the Heimlich maneuver as the primary intervention for conscious adults with complete airway obstruction.

United Hatzalah's EMTs have been reported using **back blows** for infants in news stories (e.g., EMT saving two choking victims at one emergency -- israelrescue.org/blog), but for adults, the Heimlich maneuver appears to be the standard taught technique.
