## Overview -- Electrocution in Children

Electrocution (התחשמלות) occurs when electrical current flows through the body. Children are at heightened risk due to:

- **Smaller body mass = lower resistance** -- the same voltage that causes a mild shock in an adult can cause serious injury or cardiac arrest in a child
- **Exploratory behaviour** -- toddlers frequently put objects into electrical outlets or bite/suck on electrical cords
- **Oral electrical burns** -- children under 6 commonly bite electrical cords, causing burns to the mouth, lips, and tongue that may be far deeper than they appear on the surface
- **Cardiac arrhythmia risk** -- children's hearts are more susceptible to rhythm disturbances from electrical current

### Common Scenarios in Children

- Poking metal objects (forks, keys, hairpins) into unprotected electrical outlets (שקעים)
- Biting or sucking on electrical cords (especially toddlers and infants)
- Contact with faulty electrical appliances, particularly near water (bathtub, pool)
- Contact with exposed or damaged wiring in the home
- Christmas/holiday light strings (less common in Israel but relevant)

## Injury Mechanisms

Electric shock causes three types of injury:

1. **Direct electrical damage** -- cardiac arrhythmias (הפרעות קצב לב), cardiac arrest (דום לב), respiratory paralysis (שיתוק נשימה), neurological damage
2. **Thermal burns** -- along the current pathway through the body; often internal and far more severe than the visible surface injury
3. **Traumatic injury** -- from being thrown or falling as muscles contract involuntarily

**Critical principle**: "פגיעה שהיא לכאורה קטנה ושטחית בעור, נסתרת מאחוריה פגיעה עמוקה" -- A seemingly small and superficial skin injury may conceal deep internal damage (Doctors.co.il / MDA-referenced source).

### AC vs DC

- Alternating current (זרם חילופין, standard household 220V in Israel) is generally more dangerous than direct current
- AC causes sustained muscle contraction ("lock-on" effect), making it harder for a child to let go of the source
- Exception: lightning strikes (DC) carry extreme energy

## Step-by-Step First Aid Protocol

### 1. Ensure Your Own Safety -- FIRST PRIORITY

- **Do NOT touch the child** while they are still in contact with the electrical source -- you will become an additional victim ("התחשמלות שרשרת" / chain electrocution)
- Assess the scene before approaching
- Stand on a dry, insulating surface if possible

### 2. Disconnect the Electrical Source

- **Safest action**: Turn off the main circuit breaker (מפסק ראשי) or unplug the appliance at the wall socket
- If you cannot reach the power source, use a long, dry, non-conductive object (wooden broom handle, dry wooden stick, plastic chair, dry rolled newspaper) to separate the child from the electrical source
- **Never use metal objects** or anything wet/damp
- For high-voltage situations (power lines, transformer boxes): do NOT approach. Call 101 (MDA) and 102 (Fire Services) and also the Israel Electric Corporation at 103. Maintain a safe distance.

### 3. Call 101 (MDA) Immediately

- Call Magen David Adom at **101** as soon as the child is separated from the electrical source
- Provide clearly:
  - Your phone number
  - Exact address / location
  - Your name
  - The child's approximate age
  - What happened (electrocution, type of source)
  - The child's current condition (conscious/unconscious, breathing/not breathing)
- For high-voltage incidents, also call Fire Services at **102** and the Electric Corporation at **103**
- MDA dispatchers can guide you through first aid steps while the ambulance is en route

### 4. Assess the Child

- Check responsiveness -- call the child's name, tap gently
- Check breathing -- look for chest rise, listen for breath sounds
- Check pulse

### 5. Begin CPR if Needed

- If the child is **not breathing and/or has no pulse**, begin CPR immediately
- **Child CPR (age 1-8)**: 30 compressions to 2 breaths; compress to a depth of approximately 5 cm using one or two hands on the lower half of the sternum
- **Infant CPR (under age 1)**: 30 compressions to 2 breaths; compress to a depth of approximately 4 cm using two fingers on the sternum, just below the nipple line
- Continue CPR without stopping until MDA paramedics arrive
- **Do not stop based on apparent lack of vital signs** -- electrocution victims can sometimes be resuscitated after prolonged efforts
- Use an AED (defibrillator) if one is available; pediatric pads if available, otherwise use adult pads

### 6. Treat Burns

- If the child has visible burns at the entry or exit points of the current:
  - Cool the burn with cool (not ice-cold) running water
  - Cover with sterile, non-adherent dressing
  - Do NOT apply creams, ointments, toothpaste, or home remedies
- Remember: electrical burns are often **internal** and much more severe than what is visible on the skin surface

### 7. Treat for Shock (הלם)

- Lay the child down (if conscious and no spinal injury suspected)
- Keep the child warm with a blanket
- Elevate legs slightly if no spinal injury is suspected
- Do not give food or drink

### 8. Minimise Movement

- Electrocution can cause muscle contractions that throw the child, potentially causing spinal or other traumatic injuries
- Avoid unnecessary movement of the child
- If spinal injury is suspected, keep the child still and wait for MDA

### 9. Hospital Evaluation is MANDATORY

- **Every child who experiences an electric shock -- even if it appears minor -- MUST be evaluated at a hospital**
- Internal injuries, cardiac arrhythmias, and organ damage (particularly kidneys) may not be immediately apparent
- Delayed complications can occur hours after the incident
- ECG monitoring is typically required to check for cardiac rhythm abnormalities

## Oral Electrical Burns -- Child-Specific

Children under 6 commonly bite or suck on electrical cords, causing burns to the mouth, lips, tongue, and commissure (corner of the mouth). These injuries require special attention:

- **Appearance**: may appear as a localised white or grey burn on the lip or inside the mouth
- **Hidden severity**: oral electrical burns are almost always deeper and more serious than they appear externally
- **Delayed bleeding risk**: bleeding from the labial artery can occur **hours or even days** after the initial injury -- parents must be warned about this
- **Treatment**: requires immediate medical evaluation by a pediatrician or emergency department
- Cool the area if possible; do not apply pressure to the wound
- If bleeding occurs later, apply firm pressure and call 101 (MDA) immediately

## What NOT to Do (אסור)

- **Do NOT touch the child** while they are in contact with the electrical source
- **Do NOT use metal objects or wet materials** to separate the child from the source
- **Do NOT apply ice** to electrical burns
- **Do NOT apply creams, ointments, toothpaste, or home remedies** to burns
- **Do NOT assume the child is fine** because external injuries appear minor -- internal damage may be hidden
- **Do NOT delay calling 101** -- every electrocution requires professional medical evaluation
- **Do NOT move the child unnecessarily** -- spinal and traumatic injuries may be present

## Prevention -- Israeli Context

### Electrical Outlet Safety
- Install outlet covers (מכסי שקע) on all outlets accessible to children -- this is the single most important prevention measure
- Use tamper-resistant outlets where possible
- Israeli standard outlets (Type H, 220V) are particularly dangerous as the higher voltage (compared to 110V systems) delivers more energy

### Cord Safety
- Hide or secure exposed electrical cords, especially at floor level
- Replace damaged or frayed cords immediately
- Use cord covers or cable management systems
- Keep cords away from areas where children play, crawl, or sleep

### Appliance Safety
- Never use electrical appliances near water (bathtub, sink, pool)
- Unplug appliances when not in use, especially in bathrooms and kitchens
- Keep small appliances (hair dryers, phone chargers, electric shavers) out of children's reach

### Circuit Breakers (מפסקי פחת)
- Ensure your home has a functioning residual current device (RCD / מפסק פחת)
- Test RCDs regularly by pressing the test button
- The absence of a functioning RCD significantly increases the risk of fatal electrocution

### Israeli Home Context
- Israeli homes use 220V AC at 50Hz -- this is higher voltage than North American 110V systems and poses greater risk
- Solar water heaters (דוד שמש) on rooftops can have exposed wiring -- keep children away from roof access
- Construction sites are common in Israel -- teach children to stay away from construction areas with exposed wiring
- Holiday and Shabbat electric timers and extension cords should be kept out of reach
