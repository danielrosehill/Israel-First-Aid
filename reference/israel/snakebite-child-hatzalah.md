## First Aid Protocol (from United Hatzalah materials)

### What TO Do

1. **Calm the injured person** -- reassure them and keep them still.
2. **Lie the person down** and transport to hospital (even if they feel well).
3. **Splint the affected limb** if the bite is on an arm or leg (immobilise as for a fracture).
4. **Remove constrictive items** -- rings, bracelets, watches, tight clothing from the bitten limb.
5. **Call emergency services** -- 101 (MDA) or 1221 (United Hatzalah).

### What NOT to Do

- Do NOT cut or burn the bitten area.
- Do NOT suck the bite site.
- Do NOT apply a tourniquet or block blood vessels.
- Do NOT put ice on the bite site.
- Do NOT sterilise the spot with alcohol.
- Do NOT let the injured person drink hot liquids or alcohol.

## Child-Specific Considerations

No child-specific protocol was identified from United Hatzalah sources. The general protocol above applies to all ages. For child-specific clinical considerations (faster symptom progression, higher venom-to-body-weight ratio), refer to the MDA reference file (`snakebite-child-mda.md`).

---

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

The United Hatzalah protocol is fully consistent with MDA's guidance. Both organisations agree on:

- Immobilise, calm, lie down
- Remove constrictive items
- Do NOT tourniquet, cut, suck, ice, or apply alcohol
- Transport urgently to hospital
- Call 101

**No disagreements** were identified between United Hatzalah and MDA on snakebite first aid.
