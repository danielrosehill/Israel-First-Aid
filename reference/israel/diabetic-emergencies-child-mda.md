## Background: Diabetes in Children

Type 1 diabetes (סוכרת סוג 1) is the most common form of diabetes in children. It is an autoimmune condition in which the immune system destroys the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. Children with Type 1 diabetes require lifelong insulin therapy.

The primary emergency risk for children with diabetes is **hypoglycemia** (היפוגליקמיה) -- dangerously low blood sugar. Hyperglycemia (high blood sugar) is typically managed medically rather than as a first aid situation, though severe cases (diabetic ketoacidosis / DKA) require emergency transport.

## Hypoglycemia (היפוגליקמיה) -- Low Blood Sugar

### Definition

Hypoglycemia occurs when blood glucose drops below **70 mg/dL**. For children under 6 years, treatment is recommended at values below **80 mg/dL** if symptoms are present. MDA defines hypoglycemia as a dangerous drop in blood sugar requiring immediate treatment.

### Causes

- Excessive insulin dosing (most common)
- Missed or delayed meals / insufficient carbohydrate intake
- Intense physical activity without adjusting insulin or food intake
- Vomiting or diarrhoea (malabsorption)
- Medication interactions

### Signs and Symptoms -- Child-Specific

**Stage 1 -- Compensatory response (adrenergic symptoms):**
- Paleness (חיוורון)
- Sweating (הזעה)
- Trembling / shaking (רעד)
- Rapid heartbeat (דפיקות לב)
- Anxiety and restlessness
- Hunger
- Tingling around the mouth

**Stage 2 -- Neurological effects (neuroglycopenic symptoms):**
- Confusion (טשטוש)
- General weakness (חולשה)
- Difficulty thinking / cognitive impairment
- Headaches
- **Irritability and behavioural changes** (particularly notable in young children)
- **Mood swings** (שינויי מצבי רוח קיצוניים)
- Decreased consciousness
- Seizures (פרכוסים)
- In extreme cases -- death if untreated

**Important child-specific note**: Young children, especially in gan (kindergarten) and school settings, may not be able to articulate what they are feeling. The main observable signs in young children are irritability, sudden behavioural changes, confusion, pallor, and excessive sweating. Teachers and caregivers should be alert to these signs in children known to have diabetes.

MDA states: "The main symptom indicating low blood sugar levels is decreased consciousness" (הסימן העיקרי המצביע על רמת סוכר נמוכה הוא ירידה בהכרה).

## First Aid Treatment Protocol

### Mild Hypoglycemia -- Child is Conscious and Can Swallow

**Rule of 15 (כלל ה-15):**

1. **Administer 15 grams of simple/fast-acting sugar** (for younger children under 6: 10 grams):
   - Glucose gel (גלוקוג'ל) -- 15 grams glucose syringe
   - 125 mL (half a cup) of sweetened juice (not diet)
   - 250 mL (one cup) of milk
   - 2-3 teaspoons of sugar dissolved in water
   - A sugar cube (קובית סוכר)
   - Glucose tablets (4 tablets = approximately 15 grams)
   - A teaspoon of honey (דבש)

2. **Wait 15 minutes**, then recheck blood glucose

3. **If glucose is still below target** (70-80 mg/dL depending on age), repeat the sugar dose

4. **Once glucose is above target**, provide **complex carbohydrates** to prevent recurrence:
   - A slice of bread with cheese (כריך)
   - A pastry or cracker
   - Fruit
   - Any carbohydrate-rich snack

**Important**: Avoid chocolate as the initial sugar source -- it is less effective because the fat and protein content slows sugar absorption.

### Moderate Hypoglycemia -- Child Needs Help but Can Swallow

1. Another person must assist (the child cannot self-treat)
2. Administer 20-30 grams of simple sugar via the same methods above
3. Verify glucose rises above 80 mg/dL within 10 minutes
4. If swallowing is difficult, apply **honey or glucose gel to the oral mucosa** (inside the cheek / under the tongue)
5. Follow with complex carbohydrates once glucose stabilises

### Severe Hypoglycemia -- Child is Unconscious or Seizing

**This is a life-threatening emergency.**

1. **Do NOT attempt to give food or drink by mouth** -- choking risk
2. **Disconnect the insulin pump** if the child has one -- gently disconnect without damaging the device (usually attached to the abdomen or thigh)
3. **Administer glucagon** if a kit is available and you are trained/authorised (see Glucagon section below)
4. **Place the child on their side** (recovery position) to protect the airway
5. **Call MDA immediately: 101**

## Glucagon Emergency Kit (היפו-קיט / Hypo-Kit)

### What It Is

The glucagon emergency kit contains a syringe with injection liquid and a vial of glucagon powder. Glucagon is a synthetic hormone that triggers the release of stored glucose from the liver into the bloodstream.

### Dosing for Children

| Age / Weight | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Under 6 years / under 25 kg** | Half the vial -- **0.5 mL** |
| **Over 6 years / over 25 kg** | Full vial -- **1.0 mL** |

### How to Administer

1. Draw the liquid from the syringe into the powder vial
2. Mix/shake until the solution is clear
3. Draw the mixed solution back into the syringe
4. Inject into a large muscle (intramuscular) or under the skin (subcutaneous): buttock (ישבן), abdomen (בטן), upper arm (זרוע), or thigh (ירך)

### Onset and Duration

- Effect begins approximately **10-15 minutes** after injection
- Effect lasts approximately **30 minutes**
- Use this window to provide complex carbohydrates once the child regains consciousness and can swallow

### Who May Administer Glucagon

MDA guidance notes that glucagon administration in educational settings (schools, kindergartens) is limited to **trained and authorised caregivers**. Each child discharged from hospital with a diabetes diagnosis should receive a prescription for a glucagon kit, and parents/caregivers should be trained before discharge.

**First aiders (lay responders) should call 101 (MDA) if glucagon is not available or they are not trained to use it.**

## Hyperglycemia (היפרגליקמיה) -- High Blood Sugar

### Signs and Symptoms

- Excessive thirst (צמאון)
- Frequent urination (השתנה מרובה)
- Nausea, vomiting
- Weakness, fatigue
- Abdominal pain
- Loss of appetite
- Deep, rapid breathing -- "Kussmaul breathing" (compensatory hyperventilation indicating ketoacidosis)
- Fruity-smelling breath (acetone)
- Lethargy progressing to decreased consciousness

### Causes

- Insufficient insulin (missed dose, malfunctioning pump, disconnected infusion set)
- Illness/infection causing fever
- Stress (psychological or physical)
- Spoiled or heat-damaged insulin (relevant in Israeli climate -- insulin degrades in extreme heat)
- Corticosteroid medication

### First Aid

- Hyperglycemia is typically **not managed by first aiders** -- it requires medical/pharmaceutical intervention
- **Call MDA (101)** if the child shows signs of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA): vomiting, abdominal pain, rapid deep breathing, fruity breath, decreased consciousness
- **Do NOT administer insulin** -- first aiders should never give insulin; this is a medical intervention with serious risks if done incorrectly
- Transport the child to a medical facility for evaluation

## Critical Rules for First Aiders

1. **When in doubt, treat for hypoglycemia** -- giving sugar to a hypoglycemic child is life-saving; giving sugar to a hyperglycemic child will not cause immediate harm. The reverse is not true.
2. **NEVER administer insulin** -- this is strictly a medical intervention and can be fatal if given inappropriately
3. **Always call MDA (101)** for any diabetic emergency involving loss of consciousness, seizures, or uncertainty about the child's condition
4. **Check for a medical alert bracelet or necklace** (צמיד/שרשרת רפואי/ת) that may indicate the child has diabetes
5. **Ask about an insulin pump** -- if visible, disconnect it gently in a hypoglycemic emergency

## School and Kindergarten (Gan) Context

- Children with Type 1 diabetes are common in Israeli educational settings
- Educational staff receive instruction on managing chronic conditions including diabetes (per Israeli Ministry of Health / Ministry of Education guidelines)
- High-risk children may have a glucagon kit (היפו-קיט) stored at the educational institution
- Teachers and caregivers should know the child's individual diabetes management plan
- Behavioural changes, sudden irritability, confusion, or pallor in a child known to have diabetes should prompt an immediate blood sugar check

## When to Call MDA (101)

Call Magen David Adom at **101** immediately if:

- The child **loses consciousness** or has decreased responsiveness
- The child is having **seizures**
- The child **cannot swallow** safely
- Blood glucose remains below **54 mg/dL** after 30 minutes of treatment
- **Two or more mild hypoglycemic episodes** occur within one week
- Signs of **diabetic ketoacidosis** are present (vomiting, rapid breathing, fruity breath, lethargy)
- There is **any uncertainty** about the child's condition
- Glucagon has been administered (the child needs follow-up medical care)
