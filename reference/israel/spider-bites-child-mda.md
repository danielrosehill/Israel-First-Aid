## Availability of Child-Specific Protocol

**GAP IDENTIFIED**: MDA does not publish a dedicated child-specific spider bite protocol on their public website. Israeli hospital sources (Ichilov/Tel Aviv Sourasky Medical Centre) provide general spider bite guidance. The information below synthesises Israeli medical sources.

## Venomous Spiders in Israel

### Brown Recluse Spider (Shshan Chum / ששן חום / Loxosceles rufescens)
- Mediterranean recluse spider, lives primarily indoors in Israel
- Bite usually barely felt initially
- Effects noticeable hours later
- **Local reaction**: In most cases causes local tissue damage; in minority of cases, necrosis develops at bite centre that can progress to deep ulcer with tissue damage
- **Systemic**: Rarely causes systemic illness involving clotting disorders

### Black Widow (Almana Shchora / אלמנה שחורה / Latrodectus tredecimguttatus)
- Female's bite is the dangerous one
- Reaction develops within minutes but may appear hours later
- **Symptoms**: Pain and burning at bite site, spreading with redness along lymph pathways
- **Systemic effects**: Malaise, severe abdominal pain, dyspnoea, elevated blood pressure, excessive sweating, muscle cramps spreading to abdomen, back, and chest
- Recovery typically requires 3--4 days; weakness persists for weeks
- **Antivenom is available** in Israeli hospitals

## First Aid for Spider Bites (from Israeli medical sources)

### General Treatment (All Ages)
1. Wash bite area with water and soap
2. Apply ice/cold compress to reduce pain and swelling
3. Keep the bitten limb immobilised and at heart level
4. Monitor for systemic symptoms
5. Seek medical evaluation
6. Tetanus vaccination if not current
7. **Do NOT apply tourniquet** (explicitly contraindicated for envenomation)
8. Do NOT cut or suck the wound

### Treatment Depends on Severity
- Mild: local wound care, monitoring
- Moderate: hospital evaluation, supportive care
- Severe: antivenom (for black widow), ICU care if needed

## Child-Specific Considerations

### Why Children Are More Vulnerable
- **Greater venom effect per body weight**: Same amount of venom has proportionally greater impact on smaller body mass
- Children weigh less, so venom concentration in bloodstream is higher
- Faster onset of systemic symptoms in children
- Children may not report the initial bite (often painless with brown recluse)
- Black widow effects can be more severe in children, including respiratory distress

### Paediatric Assessment
- Any spider bite in a child should receive medical evaluation
- Monitor for systemic symptoms more closely than in adults
- Watch for: severe pain, abdominal cramps, difficulty breathing, changes in consciousness
- Infants and toddlers cannot communicate symptoms -- observe for irritability, crying, poor feeding
- Hospital observation may be recommended even for apparently mild bites in young children

### Israeli Fauna Context
- Brown recluse (ששן חום) commonly found in homes, wardrobes, shoes, stored clothing
- Black widow found in gardens, stone walls, undisturbed outdoor areas
- **Deathstalker scorpion** (Leiurus quinquestriatus / עקרב צהוב) is also found in Israel and poses similar paediatric risk -- see separate reference file if available
- Prevention: shake out shoes and clothing, check bedding, clear garden debris
