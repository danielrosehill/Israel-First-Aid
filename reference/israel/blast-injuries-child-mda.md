## General MDA Blast Injury Context

### Israeli Security Context
MDA is the primary pre-hospital emergency service for all mass casualty events in Israel, including rocket/missile attacks, terrorist bombings, and military operations. MDA has extensive operational experience treating blast injuries in both adults and children.

### Blast Injury Classification (from Israeli PHTLS training)

**Primary Blast Injuries**
- Caused by pressure wave (overpressure)
- Affects air-filled organs: lungs, ears, gastrointestinal tract
- Blast lung, tympanic membrane rupture, bowel perforation

**Secondary Blast Injuries**
- Caused by flying debris, shrapnel, fragments
- Penetrating wounds, lacerations, embedded objects

**Tertiary Blast Injuries**
- Caused by victim being thrown by the blast
- Blunt trauma, fractures, head injuries

**Quaternary Blast Injuries**
- Burns, inhalation injury, crush injuries from structural collapse

### First Aid Steps (General -- all ages)
1. Ensure scene safety (secondary device risk, structural collapse)
2. Call 101 immediately
3. Follow ABCDE protocol (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure)
4. Control massive haemorrhage with tourniquet or direct pressure
5. Maintain airway patency
6. Monitor for tension pneumothorax (asymmetric chest rise)
7. Cover wounds; do NOT remove embedded objects
8. Prevent hypothermia
9. Triage if multiple casualties

## Child-Specific Considerations (from Israeli emergency medicine context)

### Why Children Are Different in Blast Injuries
- Smaller body mass means greater energy transfer per kilogram
- Organs are closer together, increasing risk of multi-organ injury
- Thinner chest wall transmits blast wave more easily
- Higher surface-area-to-mass ratio increases risk of hypothermia and burn severity
- Less blood volume means haemorrhage becomes critical faster
- Children may be closer to ground level (different shrapnel trajectory)

### Psychological Trauma
- MDA works with psychotrauma teams for paediatric psychological first aid
- Children may be non-communicative after blast -- does not mean absence of serious injury
- Keep child with parent/caregiver when possible
- MDA 101 dispatch can coordinate psychological first responders

### School and Shelter Context
- Israeli schools and kindergartens have shelters (miklat / mamad)
- Injuries may occur from shelter crowding, falling objects during rush to shelter
- MDA deploys to schools during rocket attacks

### Tourniquet Use in Children (General MDA guidance)
- MDA trains in tourniquet application as part of standard first aid
- For children, tourniquet should be applied proximal to wound
- MDA does not publish child-specific tourniquet size guidance on their public site
- See separate tourniquet reference file for details
