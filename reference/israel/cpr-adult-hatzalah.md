## Notes on Authority

United Hatzalah is Israel's largest volunteer-based EMS organisation with 8,000+ volunteers nationwide. They are **not** the authoritative national EMS provider (that role belongs to MDA), but they are a major first-responder force and often arrive on scene before MDA ambulances due to their ambucycle-based rapid response model.

**Important**: United Hatzalah does **not** publish standalone, publicly accessible CPR protocol documents on their website. Their public-facing content focuses on organisational information, rescue stories, and donation/volunteer recruitment. Specific CPR protocol details are taught through their training courses (Family Safety Course, EMT courses) but are not published online as reference documents.

## What Is Known About United Hatzalah's CPR Approach

### Training Programmes
- **Family Safety Course**: A community course teaching CPR, AED use, choking relief, and basic first aid. Designed for parents, grandparents, teachers, and babysitters. Duration not confirmed publicly.
- **EMT Course**: Full National Registry EMT certification programme.
- **NCSY Hatzalah Programme**: Teen-oriented programme teaching CPR, haemorrhage control, and first responder skills.

### CPR Protocol Alignment
Based on available evidence, United Hatzalah volunteers follow the **same AHA-based CPR guidelines as MDA**:
- 30:2 compression-to-ventilation ratio
- 100--120 compressions per minute
- At least 5 cm (2 inches) compression depth
- Early AED use
- Hands-only CPR for untrained bystanders

This alignment is expected because:
1. Both organisations operate within Israel's unified EMS framework
2. United Hatzalah volunteers coordinate with MDA dispatch and crews
3. Israeli EMT certification standards are nationally standardised
4. No published evidence suggests protocol divergence from AHA/MDA standards

### Dispatch-Assisted CPR
United Hatzalah's LifeCompass app includes:
- A **CPR instruction feature** that provides real-time step-by-step CPR guidance to callers
- A **one-tap button** to contact the United Hatzalah command centre, which stays on the line and talks the caller through performing CPR until responders arrive
- Automatic dispatch of the nearest volunteer EMTs/paramedics via GPS

## Differences from MDA (If Any)

**No protocol-level differences identified.** Both organisations follow AHA guidelines for CPR. Operational differences include:

| Aspect | MDA | United Hatzalah |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Status | National EMS (statutory authority) | Volunteer EMS (complementary) |
| Emergency number | 101 | 1221 |
| AED network | 1,000+ smart stands (remotely unlocked) | Volunteers carry personal AED kits |
| Response model | Ambulance-based | Ambucycle + on-foot rapid response |
| Dispatch-assisted CPR | Yes (T-CPR via 101) | Yes (via app and 1221 call centre) |
