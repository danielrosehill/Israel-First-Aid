## United Hatzalah's Role in Spider Bite Emergencies

United Hatzalah volunteers respond to all medical emergencies, including bites and stings. With average response times under 3 minutes, a United Hatzalah EMT may arrive before an MDA ambulance. Their dispatch centre can guide callers through first aid steps while volunteers are en route.

Spider bites are not a common emergency call type compared to cardiac events, trauma, or respiratory emergencies, but volunteers are trained to manage them as part of their Ministry of Health-certified EMT/paramedic training.

## Expected Protocol (Based on Standard Israeli EMS Training)

While no United Hatzalah-specific spider bite protocol was found online, all their volunteers follow Ministry of Health training standards. The expected protocol aligns with standard emergency medicine practice:

### Immediate First Aid

1. **Calm the patient** -- reassure them that most spider bites in Israel are not immediately life-threatening
2. **Clean the bite** with soap and water
3. **Apply ice or cold compress** wrapped in cloth -- 15 minutes on, 15 minutes off
4. **Elevate** the bitten limb
5. **Do NOT** apply tourniquet, cut the wound, or attempt suction
6. **Identify the spider if safe** -- photograph it or note appearance (do not risk another bite)

### Assessment and Escalation

United Hatzalah EMTs would assess for:
- Vital signs (pulse, blood pressure, respiratory rate)
- Local symptoms: pain, swelling, redness, discolouration, necrosis
- Systemic symptoms: muscle cramps, abdominal pain, sweating, nausea, difficulty breathing
- Risk factors: age (children and elderly at higher risk), pre-existing conditions

### When to Transport to Hospital

- Any suspected black widow (*Latrodectus tredecimguttatus*) bite -- these require medical evaluation and possible antivenom
- Systemic symptoms from any spider bite
- Progressive local symptoms (spreading redness, necrosis)
- Children, elderly, or immunocompromised patients
- Patient anxiety or uncertainty about spider identification

### Phone Guidance (Dispatch)

Callers to 1221 can expect the dispatcher to:
- Confirm the patient is safe from further bites
- Guide through wound cleaning and cold application
- Advise on whether to wait for a volunteer or go directly to hospital/urgent care
- Recommend calling the Poison Center (04-7771900) for species-specific guidance

## Medically Significant Spiders in Israel

(Same species as documented in `spider-bites-mda.md`)

### Mediterranean Recluse Spider (*Loxosceles rufescens*)
- Very common in Israel, especially in citrus groves and buildings
- Initially painless bite; progressive necrosis over days
- No antivenom available; conservative wound care
- Hundreds of bites reported for every black widow bite

### Mediterranean Black Widow (*Latrodectus tredecimguttatus*)
- Less common but potentially more dangerous systemically
- Neurotoxic venom causing muscle spasms, severe pain, autonomic symptoms
- Antivenom available for severe cases
- Hospitalise children, elderly, cardiac patients
