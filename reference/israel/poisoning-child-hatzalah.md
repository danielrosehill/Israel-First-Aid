## Definition of Poisoning (per United Hatzalah)

Poisoning is the harmful effect of any substance (toxic or non-toxic) on the body. Exposure can occur through:
- Ingestion (swallowing)
- Inhalation (breathing in fumes/gases)
- Contact with sensitive skin or mucous membranes
- Injection (rare in children)

Common substances involved: medications, drugs, cleaning products, agricultural chemicals, industrial chemicals, and even food products.

---

## Children at Highest Risk

United Hatzalah identifies children at the crawling/early walking stage as the primary risk group for ingestion poisoning. Approximately half of all poisoning victims are children under age 6. Young children explore through their mouths and show no natural aversion to dangerous smells or colours.

## First Aid Protocol: Ingestion Poisoning in Children

### Step 1: Scene Assessment
1. Ensure your own safety
2. Assess the child's consciousness, breathing, and airway
3. If unconscious or not breathing: begin CPR, call 101 or 1221

### Step 2: Identify the Substance
1. Gather all medication bottles, packaging, or containers involved
2. Collect empty packages to show medical teams
3. Note what was taken, how much, and when

### Step 3: Do NOT Induce Vomiting
United Hatzalah explicitly states that **past guidelines recommending vomiting are outdated**. Current medical understanding shows:
- Passage of substances back through the oesophagus and digestive tract can cause **additional burns**
- Risk of aspiration (inhaling stomach contents into the lungs)
- Vomiting is ineffective at removing the substance

### Step 4: Provide Supportive Care
- **For caustic substances** (cleaning products, acids, bases): if the child is conscious and breathing normally with no respiratory distress, give water or milk to dilute the substance
  - Children: approximately 1/4 to 1/2 cup (depending on age)
  - Adults: one full cup
- **For other substances**: do not give food or liquids unless instructed by the Poison Center

### Step 5: Call for Help
- **If symptoms are present** (altered consciousness, breathing difficulty, burns in/around mouth, vomiting, abdominal pain): call **101** or **1221** immediately
- **If no symptoms are present**: call the **Israel Poison Information Center at 04-7771900**

### Step 6: Transport
- All poisoning cases require hospital assessment, even if symptoms seem mild
- Some toxic substances cause no noticeable signs initially yet cause severe organ damage (liver, spleen, kidneys) that appears later
- Always bring the substance container/packaging to the hospital

## Cleaning Product Poisoning (Children) -- Detailed Protocol

United Hatzalah provides specific guidance for cleaning product exposures (highlighted during Passover cleaning season):

### Exposure to Cleaning Products by Children
- Usually does not cause significant harm, BUT there is a real risk of:
  - Burns in the oral cavity and oesophagus
  - In extreme cases: difficulty swallowing, drooling, vomiting
  - Shortness of breath, airway obstruction, choking hazard

### Warning Signs to Watch For
- Altered consciousness
- Breathing difficulty, coughing, wheezing
- Eye redness with excessive tearing
- Skin redness or burns
- Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting
- Dizziness, headache, confusion
- Neurological changes

### Treatment by Exposure Type

#### Ingestion
- **Do NOT induce vomiting**
- If conscious and breathing: give diluting fluids (water or milk, 1/4 to 1/2 cup for children)
- Maintain open airway
- Identify the chemical and bring the original container

#### Skin Contact
- Remove contaminated clothing
- Rinse affected area with running water for **15-20 minutes**

#### Eye Contact
- Flush with large amounts of running water (room temperature) for at least **15-20 minutes**
- Gently lift eyelids to ensure thorough flushing

#### Inhalation
- Move to well-ventilated area / fresh air immediately
- Do not enter confined spaces with toxic fumes without protection

## Inhalation Poisoning -- Detailed Protocol

United Hatzalah provides separate detailed guidance on inhalation poisoning:

### General Principles
- **Scene safety first**: use smoke masks or face coverings per fire department guidance
- Contact fire department (102) and dispatch center when hazardous gases, smoke, or flammable materials are suspected
- Move the victim to fresh air immediately

### Carbon Monoxide (CO) Poisoning
- **Signs**: headaches, weakness, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, possible loss of consciousness, cardiac arrhythmias, facial redness/pinkness
- **First aid**: stop exposure immediately; provide maximum concentration oxygen if available
- **Hospital treatment**: hyperbaric oxygen therapy

### Organophosphate Poisoning (Pesticides)
- **Signs**: excessive secretions (tears, saliva, sweating, respiratory fluids with "gurgling sounds"), pinpoint pupils, muscle contractions, chest pressure, breathing difficulty
- **First aid**: remove from exposure, supportive care with oxygen if available
- **Hospital treatment**: atropine and enzyme-reversing compounds

### Chemical Irritant Inhalation
- **Signs**: persistent cough, stridor (noisy breathing), difficulty swallowing, respiratory distress
- **First aid**: provide oxygen for any breathing difficulty or oxygen saturation below 94%; call for advanced help

### Critical Warning
**Never mix bleach-based cleaners with other cleaning products** (especially acids) -- this releases toxic chlorine gas causing lung irritation and asthma attacks.

## Prevention Guidance (per United Hatzalah)

### Storage and Handling
- Keep all cleaning chemicals in high, locked locations
- Never transfer products between bottles -- always use original containers
- Never mix different cleaning agents
- Ensure proper ventilation (open windows and doors) when using cleaning products
- Wear protective equipment: chemical-resistant gloves, long clothing, closed shoes, safety goggles
- Never allow children to use cleaning products
- Immediately close containers and return to secure storage after use

### Pest Control Safety
- Never place rat poison near food
- Store pesticides separately from consumables
- Follow ventilation instructions after pest treatments

---

## Things You Must NOT Do

1. **Do NOT induce vomiting** -- outdated practice, causes additional harm
2. **Do NOT give food** to a poisoned child
3. **Do NOT discard the container/packaging** -- bring it to medical teams
4. **Do NOT mix cleaning products** -- especially bleach with acids
5. **Do NOT enter a toxic fume environment** without proper protection
6. **Do NOT wait for symptoms** -- some poisons cause delayed organ damage

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

United Hatzalah's poisoning protocol is **fully aligned** with MDA and the Rambam Poison Center guidelines. Key points of agreement:
- Do NOT induce vomiting (explicitly described as outdated practice by both)
- Preserve the substance container for medical teams
- Dilute caustic substances with water or milk (same dosing: 1/4 to 1/2 cup for children)
- Call Poison Center (04-7771900) for non-symptomatic cases; call 101 for symptomatic cases
- All poisoning cases require professional assessment

**Minor differences**: United Hatzalah's dispatch number is 1221 rather than 101, but both organisations recommend calling MDA (101) for ambulance dispatch. United Hatzalah provides more detailed inhalation poisoning protocols (CO, organophosphates, cyanide) on its website, while MDA's training materials focus more on ingestion scenarios.
