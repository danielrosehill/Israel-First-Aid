## Content Extracted from Hatzalah Without Borders Article

### Definition

Electrocution (התחשמלות) is a condition in which electrical current flows through the body. Effects on the body vary depending on multiple factors, ranging from:
- Mild tingling (עקצוצים קלים) in minor cases
- Deep burns and serious damage to the nervous system, respiratory system, cardiac rhythm disturbances, and even death in severe cases

### Key Principles

- There are mild electric shocks that are frightening but not very dangerous, and then there are serious injuries from electrocution
- It is important to learn to recognise the severity so the victim receives appropriate treatment
- One must act quickly but with careful judgment and extreme caution to prevent worsening of the condition and injury to bystanders

### Child-Specific Prevention Mentioned

- Hide exposed wires
- Close electrical outlets in low-reach areas accessible to children using special covers (כיסויי שקע)
- **Oral burns in children**: "Among children, oral burns are common, as children under six years old tend to bite and suck on electrical cords" (כוויות פה נפוצות בקרב ילדים מתחת לגיל שש הנוטים לנגוס ולמצוץ כבלי חשמל)

### First Aid Steps (from search excerpts)

1. **Separate the victim from the electrical current** using a stick or the back of your hand (to prevent electrocution of the responder)
2. **Call emergency medical services** -- dial 101 (MDA) and provide your phone number, address, and name clearly
3. **Begin resuscitation if needed** -- if the victim is not breathing or has no pulse
4. **Treat burns** -- if external burns are visible, cool and wet the affected area
5. **Seek immediate medical attention** -- electrical injuries often cause internal damage that appears less severe externally

## Comparison with MDA Protocol

The Hatzalah content that was extractable is **fully consistent** with MDA guidance. No contradictions were found. The Hatzalah article provides less procedural detail than MDA-referenced sources, which is consistent with Hatzalah's role as a rapid-response volunteer organisation rather than a protocol-publishing authority.

Key alignment points:
- Scene safety first / do not touch the victim while connected to source
- Call 101 immediately
- Begin CPR if needed
- All electrocution victims require hospital evaluation
- Electrical burns are often more serious internally than externally visible

## Safe Family Programme (משפחה בטוחה)

United Hatzalah runs a "Safe Family" programme that teaches parents and caregivers basic first aid skills. The programme covers topics including CPR, choking, burns, and general medical emergencies. Electrocution is not listed as a separate topic in the programme's public-facing description, though it may be covered within the general burns or emergency response modules.
