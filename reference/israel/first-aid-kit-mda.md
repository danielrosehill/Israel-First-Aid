## Research Notes

MDA does not publish a single definitive "home first aid kit checklist" on its public website (mdais.org). The information below is synthesised from:

1. **MDA first aid course materials** -- MDA's "ne'emanei ezra rishona" (first aid trustee) course booklet (2026 edition, mdais.org/media/5577/) references first aid kit contents as part of the training curriculum but the PDF was not publicly accessible for extraction.
2. **MDA first aid articles page** -- mdais.org/101/first-aid lists first aid guidance articles but does not have a standalone kit-contents page (page returned HTTP 471 on access).
3. **MDA affiliate organisations** -- MDA Australia (magendavidadom.org.au) publishes kit contents for their branded kits.
4. **United Hatzalah shop** -- shop.1221.org.il sells a home/car first aid kit (60 ILS) but contents were not itemised on the product page.
5. **Israeli First Aid (israelifirstaid.com)** -- Sells Israeli-made kits with detailed contents lists; used as a reference for Israel-specific items.
6. **Israeli Ministry of Transportation** -- Sets the standard for vehicle first aid kits.
7. **Pikud HaOref (Home Front Command)** -- Publishes general home emergency preparedness guidance.

**Gap flagged**: No single authoritative MDA document titled "recommended home first aid kit contents" was found on the public MDA Israel website. The recommendations below are a best-effort synthesis from MDA-affiliated and official Israeli sources. If MDA publishes such a list in their course materials (which are distributed to course participants), it was not publicly accessible online as of the access date.

## Part 1: Recommended Home First Aid Kit (Adults)

### Wound Care and Bandaging

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Sterile gauze pads (5x5 cm or 10x10 cm) | 10+ | 8-layer sterile preferred |
| Rolled gauze bandage (5 cm) | 3 | For securing dressings |
| Rolled gauze bandage (10 cm) | 2 | For larger wounds |
| Israeli Emergency Bandage (4" / 6") | 1-2 | Also called "Emergency Bandage" or "abadi ishi" (personal bandage). Uniquely Israeli invention; combines primary dressing, pressure applicator, secondary dressing, and closure bar |
| Adhesive bandages (plasters/plastrim) assorted sizes | 20+ | "Silk Touch" elastic cotton preferred |
| Butterfly closure strips (steri-strips) | 1 pack | For wound closure |
| Triangular bandage (meshulash) | 2 | With safety pins; for slings and immobilisation |
| Elastic bandage (6 cm) | 1 | For sprains and compression |
| Hypoallergenic medical tape (micropore) | 1 roll | 2.5 cm x 5-9 m |
| Non-adherent wound dressing | 5 | For burns and abrasions |

### Bleeding Control

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Hemostatic gauze pads (e.g., SIDA-CEL, Celox) | 2-3 | Israeli-made hemostatic products widely available |
| Hemostatic agent packets (e.g., Celox Home 2g) | 3 | For minor to moderate bleeding |
| Compressed gauze for wound packing (e.g., Tytek EZ-Gauze) | 1 | For deep wounds requiring packing |

### Burn Treatment

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Burn dressing (e.g., Burnshield 4"x4") | 3 | Gel-impregnated burn dressings |
| Burn gel sachets | 3-5 | For immediate cooling of burns |

### Tools and Equipment

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Trauma shears (7.5") | 1 | For cutting clothing, bandages, tape |
| Tweezers (plastic or stainless steel) | 1 | For splinter/debris removal |
| Medical thermometer (digital) | 1 | Oral or forehead type |
| Blood pressure cuff (manual or digital) | 1 | Optional but recommended |
| Pulse oximeter | 1 | Optional; useful for respiratory emergencies |
| Pen torch / flashlight | 1 | For pupil checks and visibility |
| Permanent marker (Sharpie) | 1 | For marking tourniquet time, notes on skin |
| Safety pins | 6 | For securing bandages and slings |

### Infection Prevention

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Nitrile examination gloves (pairs) | 5+ | Latex-free preferred |
| Alcohol prep pads (70% isopropyl) | 10+ | For disinfection |
| Antiseptic solution (Polydine/povidone-iodine) | 1 bottle | Widely available in Israeli pharmacies |
| Antibacterial hand sanitiser | 1 small bottle | |
| Sterile eye wash ampoules | 2-3 | For eye irrigation |

### Respiratory and CPR

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|

| Emergency rescue blanket (silver/gold) | 1-2 | 150x210 cm; for shock, hypothermia, heat |

### Immobilisation

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|

| SAM splint (36" full size) | 1 | Optional; for arm/leg fractures |

## Part 2: Israel-Specific Security/Trauma Items (Civilian Trauma Kit)

Given Israel's security context, many households -- particularly those near border areas or in settlements -- maintain additional trauma supplies. These items are commonly taught in MDA and United Hatzalah civilian first aid courses.

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Combat Application Tourniquet (CAT) or SAM-XT | 1-2 | For life-threatening limb haemorrhage. SAM-XT is Israeli-made. Practice with the specific model you own |
| Israeli Emergency Bandage (6") | 2 | The larger size for significant wounds |
| Chest seal (vented, e.g., HyFin or SAM) | 1 pair | For penetrating chest wounds (2 seals: entry + exit) |
| Hemostatic gauze (combat gauze / Celox / SIDA-CEL) | 2 | Z-fold for wound packing |
| Compressed gauze for wound packing | 2 | |
| NPA (nasopharyngeal airway) | 1 | Only if trained in use |
| TCCC (Tactical Combat Casualty Care) card | 1 | English or Hebrew version |
| Permanent marker | 1 | For recording tourniquet application time |
| Trauma shears | 1 | |

**Source**: Israeli First Aid tactical kit (israelifirstaid.com) and MDA/United Hatzalah civilian "Stop the Bleed" training curriculum.

## Part 3: Items for Families with Children

### Pediatric-Specific Equipment

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Pediatric CPR mask (infant size) | 1 | Smaller mask for infant/child rescue breathing |
| Digital thermometer (rectal for infants) | 1 | Most accurate for children under 3 |
| Infant nasal aspirator | 1 | For clearing blocked airways |
| Oral rehydration salts (ORS) sachets | 5-10 | Critical for dehydration from gastro illness; available at any pharmacy |
| Child-sized bandages (small plasters) | 20+ | Colourful/character designs help with compliance |
| Paediatric dosing syringe/cup | 1 | For accurate medication dosing |

### Medications for Children (OTC in Israel)

| Medication | Hebrew/Brand Name | Notes |
|------------|------------------|-------|
| Paracetamol (children's liquid) | Acamol Tsinuk / Acamol Li | Weight-based dosing; available at all pharmacies |
| Ibuprofen (children's liquid) | Advil / Nurofen for Children | From age 3 months (by weight) |
| Antihistamine (children's liquid) | Telfast / Fenistil drops | For allergic reactions |
| Saline nasal drops | Physiomer / saline drops | For nasal congestion in infants |

### EpiPen for Families

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| EpiPen (0.3 mg) | For adults/children over 30 kg. Prescription required in Israel |
| EpiPen Junior (0.15 mg) | For children 15-30 kg. Prescription required in Israel |

**Note**: EpiPens in Israel require a prescription and are covered by kupot cholim (health funds) for patients with diagnosed allergies. Store at room temperature (15-25C); do not refrigerate. Check expiry dates regularly -- EpiPens have a relatively short shelf life (12-18 months).

## Part 4: Climate-Specific Items for Israel

Israel's climate -- Mediterranean coast, arid Negev desert, humid Jordan Valley -- creates specific first aid needs.

### Heat and Sun Protection

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Electrolyte sachets (e.g., Slow Sodium, ORS) | For heat exhaustion and dehydration; dissolve in water |
| Sunscreen (SPF 50+) | Essential year-round in Israel |
| Aloe vera gel | For sunburn treatment |
| Cooling towels | Wet and apply for heat exhaustion |
| Extra bottled water (2L per person per day) | Per Pikud HaOref (Home Front Command) recommendation |

### Insect and Animal Stings/Bites

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Sting relief pads / antihistamine cream | For bee, wasp, and insect stings |
| Cold packs (instant) | For swelling from stings |
| Antihistamine tablets (Telfast/cetirizine) | Oral antihistamine for allergic reactions |
| Elastic bandage (pressure immobilisation) | For suspected snakebite (Vipera palaestinae) -- immobilise, do NOT apply tourniquet |
| Tweezers | For tick removal |

**Important**: For scorpion stings (deathstalker/Leiurus quinquestriatus) and snakebites (Palestine viper/Vipera palaestinae), the primary treatment is to call MDA (101) immediately and transport to hospital for antivenom. Home first aid is supportive only.

## Part 5: Vehicle First Aid Kit (Israeli Law)

### Legal Requirements

A first aid kit in a private vehicle is **recommended but not legally mandatory** for private cars in Israel. It **is mandatory** for:

- Security and emergency vehicles
- Trucks
- Shuttle/public transport vehicles (minibuses, buses)

Despite not being a legal requirement for private cars, MDA and the Ministry of Transportation strongly recommend carrying one.

### Ministry of Transportation Standard Contents

The Israeli Ministry of Transportation (Misrad HaTachbura) specifies the following minimum contents for vehicle first aid kits:

| Item | Quantity |
|------|----------|
| Personal bandage (sterile, military model / Israeli bandage) | 1 |
| Triangular bandage (for immobilisation/sling) | 1 |
| Adhesive bandages (plasters) | 10 |
| Sterile gauze strip | 1 |
| Binding bandage (6 cm) | 1 |
| Bandage scissors | 1 |

### Recommended Additional Items for Vehicle Kit

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Nitrile gloves (2 pairs) | |
| Burn dressing | |
| Emergency blanket | |
| Emergency light stick (green, 6") | For visibility at night |
| CPR mask | |
| Reflective safety vest | Legally required in vehicle |
| Bottled water (0.5L) | For wound irrigation |

### Storage and Maintenance

- Store the kit in the glove compartment or door pocket -- **not in the boot/trunk** (must be quickly accessible)
- The bag should be sealed and resistant to heat, moisture, and humidity
- Check contents every **6 months**
- General kit validity: **5 years**, but check individual item expiry dates
- Israeli summers can reach 40C+; extreme heat degrades medications and adhesives faster

## Part 8: Expiry Date Tracking

### Recommendations

1. **Create an inventory list** -- tape it inside the kit lid or store digitally
2. **Check every 6 months** -- set calendar reminders for Rosh Hashana and Pesach as natural checkpoints
3. **Items with shortest shelf life**:
   - EpiPen: 12-18 months
   - Medications (Acamol, Optalgin, etc.): typically 2-3 years
   - Sterile dressings: 3-5 years (shorter if seal is broken)
   - Adhesive bandages: 3-5 years (adhesive degrades in heat)
   - Burn gel: 3-4 years
   - Hemostatic gauze: 3-5 years
   - Antiseptic wipes/pads: 2-3 years
   - Nitrile gloves: 3-5 years
4. **Heat accelerates degradation** -- Israeli summers (35-45C) shorten shelf life of adhesives, gels, and medications stored in cars or non-climate-controlled spaces
5. **Replace used items immediately** -- do not wait for the 6-month check
6. **Vehicle kits degrade faster** -- check more frequently (every 3-4 months) due to heat exposure
