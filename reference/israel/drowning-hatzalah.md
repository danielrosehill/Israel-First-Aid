## United Hatzalah Drowning Guidance

### Emergency response

1. **Call emergency services immediately** if you see someone drowning or in distress in or near water
2. **Begin CPR** if the person is not breathing, and continue until emergency services arrive
3. **Locate the nearest AED (defibrillator)** and bring it to the patient
   - **Critical**: Dry the patient and move them to a dry place before using the AED device

### Secondary drowning (טביעה משנית)

United Hatzalah published a dedicated article and video (in collaboration with the National Council for Drowning Prevention) about secondary drowning:

- Secondary drowning is a delayed complication that can occur **hours after** a water incident
- Even if a person appears to have recovered from a near-drowning event, dangerous symptoms can develop later
- **Always seek hospital evaluation** after any near-drowning incident
- The article directs readers to an embedded video for detailed medical explanation of the mechanism

### Water safety guidelines

United Hatzalah emphasises the following prevention measures:

- **Swim only at declared beaches** during lifeguard duty hours
- **Constant child supervision**: Children must remain under continuous adult watch, within immediate arm's reach
- **No phone distractions**: Adults supervising children near water must avoid using mobile phones
- **Afternoon wind awareness**: Pay special attention to changing water conditions, particularly afternoon hours when western winds create strong currents (Mediterranean coast)
- **No alcohol**: Never enter water after consuming alcoholic beverages

### Incident response capability

United Hatzalah's volunteer network includes:

- Over 8,000 volunteer EMTs, paramedics, and doctors across Israel
- Average response time of **90 seconds** in urban areas
- Volunteers carry personal emergency medical equipment including resuscitation gear
- In drowning incidents documented by United Hatzalah, responders perform alternating CPR compressions and assisted ventilation, with advanced procedures (sedation, intubation) performed upon arrival of mobile intensive care ambulance units

---

## Alignment with MDA

United Hatzalah's drowning guidance is consistent with MDA protocols. Key points of alignment:

- Immediate CPR for non-breathing victims
- Emphasis on calling 101 (MDA) for national EMS dispatch
- AED use with dry-patient precaution
- Mandatory hospital transport after any near-drowning event
- Prevention focus on supervised swimming at official beaches

There are **no notable disagreements** between United Hatzalah and MDA on drowning first aid for adults.
