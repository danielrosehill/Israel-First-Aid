# Heart Attack / Chest Pain — United Hatzalah Reference

## Source: United Hatzalah (איחוד הצלה)

- **Organisation**: United Hatzalah of Israel — volunteer-based emergency medical services organisation
- **Website**: https://israelrescue.org/ (English) / https://1221.org.il/ (Hebrew)
- **Emergency number**: 1221
- **Date accessed**: 2026-03-15
- **Authority**: United Hatzalah is a licensed, Ministry of Health-supervised EMS organisation. All its volunteers are certified as EMTs, paramedics, or physicians by Israeli medical training authorities. However, MDA (not United Hatzalah) is the statutory national EMS provider, so MDA protocols take precedence where they differ.

---

## Findings

United Hatzalah does **not publish detailed, step-by-step first aid protocols for heart attack on their public website** (israelrescue.org). Their public content focuses on:

- Organisational information and volunteer recruitment
- Response time statistics (target: under 3 minutes in urban areas)
- The EZ-CPR mobile application for bystander CPR guidance
- General information about cardiac arrest response

### What is available from United Hatzalah sources:

1. **Response model**: United Hatzalah dispatches the nearest available volunteer first responder (often on an ambucycle) to reach the patient within minutes. For cardiac events, this rapid response is critical — they cite that survival drops by approximately 10% for each minute without CPR after cardiac arrest.

2. **EZ-CPR App**: United Hatzalah developed a free mobile application that:
   - Provides real-time CPR instructions to bystanders
   - Connects the user to United Hatzalah's medical command centre
   - Simultaneously dispatches nearby volunteer EMTs, paramedics, and doctors
   - Available for download on iOS and Android

3. **Training**: United Hatzalah volunteers are trained and certified under Ministry of Health supervision. Their cardiac emergency protocols are aligned with Israeli national standards (which are set in coordination with MDA and the Ministry of Health).

4. **CPR emphasis**: United Hatzalah strongly emphasises that brain death begins 4-6 minutes after cardiac arrest, making bystander intervention in those first minutes critical.

## Comparison with MDA

| Aspect | MDA | United Hatzalah |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Emergency number | 101 | 1221 |
| Status | Statutory national EMS | Licensed volunteer EMS |
| Public first aid guides | Available (Hebrew, some English) | Not published in detail online |
| Response model | Dispatches ambulance + MICU | Dispatches nearest volunteer on ambucycle |
| Protocol authority | Sets national protocols | Follows national protocols |
| Heart attack protocol | Detailed (see heart-attack-mda.md) | Not independently published |

**Recommendation**: For heart attack first aid protocol content, rely on MDA guidance (see `heart-attack-mda.md`). United Hatzalah's clinical protocols are consistent with MDA's but are not published for public consumption. In practice, calling both 101 (MDA) and 1221 (United Hatzalah) during a cardiac emergency is advisable, as the nearest United Hatzalah volunteer may arrive faster.

## Gap Assessment

- **No public heart attack first aid protocol found** on israelrescue.org or 1221.org.il.
- United Hatzalah's value in cardiac emergencies is primarily in their rapid-response dispatch model and the EZ-CPR app, rather than in publishing lay-person protocols.
- Their internal clinical protocols (used by trained volunteers) are aligned with MDA/Ministry of Health standards but are not available online for reference.
