## Definitions — Dehydration vs Heat Stroke

### Dehydration (Hit'yabshut / התייבשות)
Occurs when the body loses more fluids than it consumes. Fluid loss happens primarily through sweating and urination. **The person continues sweating.** Temperature is normal or slightly elevated.

### Heat Exhaustion (Hit'ayshut Khom / התעייפות חום)
Precursor to heat stroke. The body overheats due to hot weather or excessive physical exertion causing profuse sweating.

### Heat Stroke (Makat Khom / מכת חום)
**Medical emergency.** Body temperature regulation fails. Temperature rises above 40 degrees C. **Sweating STOPS** — this is a critical distinguishing sign. Internal organs begin to be damaged (heart, brain, kidneys, muscles).

## Recognition — Signs of Dehydration

### Mild Dehydration
- Increased thirst
- Dry mouth
- Reduced urination or dark-coloured urine
- Fatigue and general weakness
- Skin flushing

### Moderate Dehydration
- All mild symptoms plus:
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Headaches
- Severe dry mouth
- Rapid pulse
- Nausea and restlessness

### Severe Dehydration / Heat Stroke — CALL 101
- Body temperature exceeding 40 degrees C
- Hot, dry, reddened skin (no sweating)
- Rapid, forceful pulse
- Confusion, restlessness, unclear speech
- Extreme fatigue, severe headache
- Nausea, vomiting
- Loss of consciousness

## First Aid Steps

### For Dehydration (Mild to Moderate)

1. **Stop all activity** — ensure complete rest
2. **Move to shade and cool place** — air-conditioned room if available
3. **Rehydrate gradually** — small sips of water or ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution)
   - **Do NOT drink rapidly** — rapid fluid replacement can worsen symptoms due to electrolyte imbalance and may increase nausea and vomiting
   - Use a straw for small sips if needed
   - ORS is preferred over plain water for moderate dehydration
4. **Remove excess clothing** — loosen tight garments
5. **Cool the body** — wet cloths, fan, cool (not cold) water spray
6. **Call MDA at 101** if symptoms do not improve or worsen

### For Heat Stroke — EMERGENCY

1. **Call MDA at 101 immediately**
2. **Move to a shaded, ventilated, or air-conditioned location**
3. **Remove excess clothing**
4. **Cool the body rapidly** — ice, wet towels, fans, cold water on torso
5. **If conscious**: offer small sips of water only
6. **If unconscious**: lay on back, elevate legs, place in recovery position if breathing
7. **Do NOT wet the head** (to prevent choking risk if consciousness is altered)
8. **Continue active cooling until MDA arrives**

### ORS — Oral Rehydration Solution (Tmusat Mikhlakhim / תמוסת מכלחים)

For moderate dehydration, ORS replaces both fluids and electrolytes:
- Available at Israeli pharmacies (e.g., Electrolit, Hydralite)
- Can be made at home: 1 litre of boiled/clean water + 6 teaspoons sugar + 0.5 teaspoon salt
- Administer in small, frequent sips
- Do not give sugary drinks, coffee, or alcohol

## When to Call MDA (101) Immediately

- Body temperature above 40 degrees C
- Sweating has stopped despite hot environment
- Confusion, disorientation, or loss of consciousness
- Seizures
- Rapid pulse with low blood pressure
- No improvement after 30 minutes of rest and rehydration
- Vomiting prevents oral rehydration
- Signs of shock: pale, clammy skin, rapid breathing

## Dehydration vs Heat Stroke — Key Distinction

| Feature | Dehydration | Heat Stroke |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Sweating | Present | ABSENT |
| Temperature | Normal or slightly elevated | Above 40 degrees C |
| Consciousness | Usually maintained | Often altered |
| Skin | May be flushed | Hot, dry, red |
| Severity | Can be managed with fluids | Medical emergency |

## Israeli Context

- **Extreme heat**: Temperatures regularly exceed 40 degrees C in the Negev, Arava, and Jordan Valley during summer (June-September). Even coastal cities like Tel Aviv reach 35+ degrees C.
- **Hiking risks**: Popular trails in the Negev (Makhtesh Ramon, Ein Avdat), Judean Desert (Ein Gedi, Masada), and Dead Sea area are high-risk for dehydration. The Israel Nature and Parks Authority (INPA) issues heat warnings and sometimes closes trails.
- **Military training**: IDF heat casualties are a known risk; soldiers are trained in buddy-system hydration monitoring.
- **Ramadan fasting**: Muslims fasting during Ramadan in summer months are at increased dehydration risk, especially outdoor workers. Breaking the fast should include gradual rehydration.
- **Construction workers**: Outdoor labourers in Israel's summers are a high-risk group.
- **Elderly and children**: Particular risk during khamsin (sharav) heat waves.
- **Water availability**: Israel has excellent tap water quality; hikers should carry 1 litre per hour of hiking in summer heat.

## High-Risk Groups

- Infants and young children
- Elderly individuals (over 65)
- Athletes and outdoor workers
- Pregnant women
- People with chronic conditions (heart disease, diabetes, kidney disease)
- People taking diuretics or certain medications
