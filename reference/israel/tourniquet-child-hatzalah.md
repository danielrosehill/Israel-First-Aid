## Tourniquet Protocol (from Ichud Hatzalah)

### General Tourniquet Guidance
- Tourniquet (חוסם עורקים / חסם עורקים) is used to stop severe, life-threatening limb haemorrhage
- Part of the C (Circulation) step in ABCDE trauma assessment
- Applied when direct pressure is insufficient

### Equipment Available (from Hatzalah Shop)
- **Basic rubber tourniquet**: Simple elastic design for stopping bleeding during limb injuries
- **CAT SAM XT (original)**: Essential emergency tool with simple one-handed operation; "chosen by security and rescue forces in Israel and worldwide"
- **CAT Standard (compatible)**: Used by Ichud Hatzalah volunteers; "built on lessons and extensive experience in treating severe combat and emergency injuries"

### Case Study: Tourniquet in Blast Injury (from Missile Strike article)
- Improvised tourniquet (חוסם עורקים מאולתר) successfully applied to control significant femoral artery bleeding from open fracture
- Emphasises that tourniquet can be improvised when manufactured devices unavailable

### Important Contraindication
- For venomous animal bites (snakes, scorpions, spiders): "do NOT apply tourniquets, do not bandage, do not cut, do not suck, do not cool" -- tourniquets worsen envenomation outcomes

## Child-Specific Tourniquet Information

### From Ichud Hatzalah
**GAP IDENTIFIED**: Ichud Hatzalah does not publish paediatric-specific tourniquet guidance on their website. Their tourniquet products and training appear to be oriented toward adult/general use.

### From International Evidence (applicable to Israeli context)

**CAT Gen 7 for Children**
- Effective for most school-age children (approximately age 2+ depending on limb size)
- Success in limbs with circumferences approximately 13--16 cm and above
- Failed on very small paediatric mannequin (5.08 cm diameter)

**Children Under 2 / Very Small Limbs**
- No manufactured windlass tourniquet reliably effective
- SWAT-T and RATS tourniquets performed best across all paediatric sizes in NAEMSP study
- For infants: direct pressure is the primary haemorrhage control method
- Improvised tourniquet (as described in the missile strike case study) may be necessary

**Key Paediatric Principles**
- Children have less blood volume and decompensate rapidly from haemorrhage
- Apply with same urgency as adults
- Record time of application
- Do NOT remove in the field
- Transport urgently

### Limb Circumference Guide (approximate)
- CAT: from approximately 13 cm circumference upward
- SWAT-T/RATS: effective from smallest sizes
- Below 13 cm: elastic-type tourniquet or direct pressure with wound packing
