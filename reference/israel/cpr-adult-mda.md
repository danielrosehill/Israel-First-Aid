## Adult CPR Protocol (Bystander / First Responder)

### Recognition

1. **Check responsiveness** -- Call out to the person and speak to them. Shake their shoulders gently and ask loudly: "Are you okay?" (Hebrew: "Ha'im ata beseder?")
2. **Test response to touch** -- Apply gentle stimulation (e.g., a light pinch) to determine if they respond to physical contact.
3. **Check breathing** -- Look for chest rise, listen for breath sounds, feel for air on your cheek. Check for no more than **10 seconds**. Gasping (agonal breathing) is NOT normal breathing -- treat as cardiac arrest.

### Activate Emergency Response

4. **Call 101** (MDA emergency dispatch) immediately if the person is unresponsive and not breathing normally.
   - If you are with someone else: have them call 101 while you begin CPR.
   - If you are alone: call 101 first, then retrieve an AED if nearby, then begin CPR.
   - Exception: if choking is suspected, perform CPR first, then call.
5. **Request or locate an AED** -- By Israeli law, any public space with a capacity of 500+ people must have a defibrillator. MDA operates 1,000+ "smart stands" that can be remotely unlocked by the 101 dispatch centre.
   - Use the **Eifo-Defi** ("Where's the Defib?") app to find the nearest AED.

### Chest Compressions

6. **Position the person** supine (face up) on a **firm, flat surface**.
7. **Hand placement**: Place the heel of one hand in the **centre of the chest, on the breastbone, between the nipples**. Place your other hand on top, interlocking fingers.
8. **Compress**:
   - **Rate**: 100--120 compressions per minute
   - **Depth**: At least **5 cm (2 inches)**, not exceeding **6 cm (2.4 inches)**
   - **Technique**: Use upper body weight; press straight down; keep arms locked and straight
   - **Full recoil**: Allow the chest to fully return to its normal position between compressions -- do not lean on the chest
   - **Minimise interruptions**: Keep pauses to under 10 seconds

### Ventilation (Trained Rescuers)

9. **Ratio**: **30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths** per cycle
10. **Open the airway**: Tilt the head back gently and lift the chin (head-tilt/chin-lift manoeuvre).
11. **Deliver rescue breaths**: Pinch the nose closed, seal your mouth over theirs, and deliver each breath over **1 second** with enough volume to produce **visible chest rise**. Do not over-ventilate.
12. **Resume compressions** immediately after the 2 breaths.

### Hands-Only CPR (Untrained Bystanders)

If you are not trained in CPR or are unwilling/unable to perform rescue breaths:

- Perform **continuous chest compressions** at 100--120 per minute without stopping for breaths.
- This is the recommended approach for untrained bystanders witnessing an adult sudden collapse.

### AED Use

13. **Apply the AED as soon as it is available** -- do not delay.
14. **Electrode placement**:
    - One pad: under the right collarbone
    - Other pad: under and to the left of the left breast (or one front, one back)
15. **Follow the AED's voice prompts** -- the device will analyse the rhythm and advise whether a shock is indicated.
16. **Ensure no one is touching the person** during analysis and shock delivery.
17. **Resume CPR immediately** after shock delivery (or if no shock advised) -- do not wait for the AED to re-analyse.

### Reassessment and Continuation

18. **Reassess every 2 minutes** (approximately 5 cycles of 30:2).
19. **Rotate compressors every 2 minutes** if two or more trained rescuers are present, to maintain compression quality. Swap in under 10 seconds.
20. **Continue CPR** until:
    - EMS (MDA) arrives and takes over
    - The person shows definite signs of life (breathing, movement, coughing)
    - An AED prompts you to stop
    - You are physically unable to continue
21. **Recovery position**: If the person begins breathing spontaneously, place them on their side (recovery position) -- upper leg and hip bent at 90 degrees -- to maintain the airway and prevent aspiration if they vomit.

## Israel-Specific Considerations

### Emergency Infrastructure
- **Emergency number**: 101 (MDA) -- dispatchers provide real-time CPR coaching over the phone (T-CPR / telephone-assisted CPR)
- **AED legal mandate**: Israeli law requires AEDs in public spaces accommodating 500+ people
- **Smart stands**: MDA's 1,000+ remotely unlocked AED stations in malls, transit hubs, parks, government buildings, sports facilities
- **Eifo-Defi app**: Crowdsourced AED location finder

### Climate Considerations
- In extreme heat (common in summer, especially Negev/Jordan Valley), cardiac events may be more frequent due to dehydration and heat stress
- Move the person to shade if possible before starting CPR, but do not delay compressions
- Be aware that hot surfaces (asphalt, sand) can cause burns -- place a barrier under the person if feasible

### Legal Protection
- Israel has a "Good Samaritan" provision encouraging bystander intervention
- AED use by laypeople is legally supported

### Training Availability
- MDA offers a **Basic Resuscitation Course** (CPR + AED) -- see https://www.mdais.org/kursim/basic-resuscitation-course
- MDA's courses are **AHA-certified**
- Course types range from 4-hour community courses to 227-hour EMT certification

## Gaps and Limitations

1. **MDA's official BLS/ALS protocol PDFs** (blsguide2016.pdf, als-2024.pdf) are in Hebrew and behind access restrictions that prevented direct content extraction. The protocol above is reconstructed from MDA's public CPR page, aligned Israeli health sources, and the AHA guidelines that MDA officially follows.
2. **No standalone English-language MDA CPR protocol document** was found publicly available. MDA's training materials are primarily in Hebrew.
3. **The BLS guide on MDA's site dates from 2016**; the ALS guide was updated in April 2024. MDA's adoption of the 2025 AHA guidelines has not yet been confirmed in a public document, though their longstanding AHA partnership makes adoption highly likely.
4. **Israel does not have a separate national resuscitation council** -- MDA serves this function and defers to AHA/ILCOR consensus.
