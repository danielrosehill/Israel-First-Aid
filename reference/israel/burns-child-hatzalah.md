## Findings

**United Hatzalah does not publish detailed burns first aid protocols on their public-facing website.**

Their website (israelrescue.org) focuses on:
- Organisational mission and volunteer recruitment
- Psychotrauma and crisis response programmes
- The IMAH (Israeli Mothers at Home) initiative for family safety training
- Donation and fundraising
- News and event coverage

United Hatzalah offers a "Family Safety Course" that covers first aid for parents, grandparents, teachers, and babysitters, but the specific course content (including burn treatment protocols) is not published online. The course materials are delivered in-person during training sessions.

## Assessment

United Hatzalah's operational model is focused on rapid first-responder dispatch (average response time under 3 minutes) rather than public-facing protocol documentation. Their EMTs and paramedics follow protocols that are broadly consistent with MDA standards, as both organisations operate under Israeli medical regulatory oversight.

For publicly available Israeli burn first aid protocols, **MDA (mdais.org) is the authoritative source**. See `burns-child-mda.md` in this directory for the synthesised Israeli protocol.

## Recommendation

If United Hatzalah-specific protocol content is needed, options include:
1. Attending a United Hatzalah Family Safety Course in person
2. Contacting United Hatzalah directly for training materials
3. Checking the Hebrew-language site (1221.org.il) for any updated educational content
