## 1. Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

### Key Point from United Hatzalah

United Hatzalah emphasises that hypoglycemia is NOT exclusive to diabetic patients. Their article specifically addresses hypoglycemia in the general (non-diabetic) population, noting that it can also be caused by:
- Kidney dysfunction (impaired insulin clearance)
- Liver disease (reduced glycogen storage)
- Adrenal and pituitary disorders (disrupted hormonal cascade)
- Insulinoma (pancreatic tumour causing excess insulin secretion)

This is a notable educational emphasis -- United Hatzalah volunteers are trained to test glucose in ALL patients with altered consciousness, not only known diabetics.

### Recognition and Assessment

**"The most significant indicator of reduced blood glucose is decreased consciousness"** -- United Hatzalah training material.

Symptoms appear in two stages:

#### Stage 1: Compensatory (Adrenergic) Response
- Pallor
- Sweating
- Tremors
- Anxiety
- Rapid heart rate

#### Stage 2: Neurological (Neuroglycopenic) Symptoms
- Confusion
- Weakness
- Headache
- Behavioural changes
- Seizures/convulsions
- Loss of consciousness

### First Aid Treatment

#### Conscious patient:
1. Administer rapidly-absorbed sugars:
   - **Glucogel packets** (15g glucose) -- standard in United Hatzalah volunteer kits
   - Alternatively: honey, fruit juice, or milk
2. After initial stabilisation, provide complex carbohydrates to maintain glucose levels.
3. Monitor patient and reassess.

#### Unconscious or non-responsive patient:
- **DO NOT give food or liquids by mouth** -- choking risk.
- Call 101 (MDA) / 1221 for advanced care.
- Paramedics will administer intravenous dextrose.
- Take seizure precautions (protect the patient from injury, do not restrain).

#### Insulin pump users:
- **Disconnect the insulin pump** during a hypoglycaemic episode.
- Do so gently to avoid damaging the device.
- Glucagon kits may be available and administered by trained caregivers; United Hatzalah volunteers should recognise these kits.

## 2. Hyperglycemia (High Blood Sugar)

### Signs and Symptoms
(From Hatzalah Without Borders hyperglycemia guide)
- Excessive thirst and frequent urination
- Fruity/acetone breath odour
- Fast, deep breathing
- Nausea and vomiting
- Warm, dry skin
- Weakness and fatigue
- Confusion progressing to loss of consciousness

### First Aid Treatment
1. **Call 101 / 1221 immediately** if diabetic ketoacidosis is suspected.
2. Ensure airway is open.
3. Loosen restrictive clothing.
4. Allow water intake if patient is conscious.
5. **DO NOT administer insulin** -- this requires medical professional assessment.
6. Place in recovery position if consciousness is lost.

## 3. United Hatzalah Volunteer Equipment

United Hatzalah volunteers carry glucometers as standard equipment in their medical kits. This enables rapid blood glucose testing at the scene, which is critical because:
- Altered consciousness in a diabetic patient could be either hypo- or hyperglycemia.
- Correct identification determines the appropriate first aid response.
- Glucose testing should also be performed on any patient with unexplained altered consciousness (to rule out hypoglycemia even in non-diabetic patients).

---

## 4. Comparison with MDA Protocol

United Hatzalah protocols are broadly consistent with MDA guidelines. Notable points of alignment:
- Both emphasise glucogel/sugar administration for conscious hypoglycaemic patients.
- Both prohibit food/drink for unconscious patients.
- Both instruct first aiders NOT to give insulin.
- Both call for immediate EMS activation (101) in severe cases.

**Notable United Hatzalah emphasis that complements MDA**:
- Stronger emphasis on hypoglycemia in non-diabetic populations (kidney, liver, adrenal causes).
- Explicit mention of insulin pump disconnection protocol.
- Glucogel packets specifically named as standard volunteer equipment.
- Glucagon kit recognition (though administration is by trained caregivers, not basic first aiders).
