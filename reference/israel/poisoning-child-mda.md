## Key Statistics (Israel)

- Approximately 90% of child poisoning incidents occur at home when children lack supervision
- The Israel Poison Information Center receives over 37,000 calls per year
- 43% of calls concern children under age 6
- 49% of poisoning exposures involve medications
- 36% involve chemicals (cleaning products, pesticides)
- 5% involve poisonous plants or venomous animals
- Approximately 77% of pediatric poisoning cases do not require emergency department care (but all should be assessed by phone at minimum)

## Routes of Poisoning

| Route | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Ingestion (most common in children) | Medications, cleaning products, plants, pesticides |
| Inhalation | Toxic fumes, gas leaks, mixing cleaning products |
| Skin/mucous membrane contact | Caustic chemicals, pesticides |
| Eye contact | Splashed cleaning products, sprays |
| Injection | Rare in children; insect stings/snake bites covered separately |

## Common Substances Involved in Child Poisoning in Israel

### Medications (~40-49% of cases)
- Over-the-counter pain relievers (paracetamol/acamol, ibuprofen/nurofen)
- Prescription medications left accessible
- Vitamins and supplements (especially iron-containing)
- Herbal remedies

### Cleaning Products (~36% of cases)
- Bleach (economica / akonomiqa)
- Drain cleaners (acids)
- Oven cleaners (bases/alkalis)
- Dishwasher tablets and laundry pods (particularly attractive to toddlers due to colourful appearance)
- Limescale removers

### Pesticides and Household Chemicals
- Insecticides
- Rodent poisons
- Pest control residues

### Plants
- Various garden and houseplants (approximately 5% of cases)

### Other Hazards
- Alcohol (causes severe hypoglycemia, dehydration, and blood pressure drops in young children)
- Adhesive inhalants and aerosol propellants (older children/teens)
- Cannabis edibles (increasing trend per Rambam Poison Center reports)

### Seasonal Context: Passover (Pesach)
Intensive house cleaning before Passover leads to a spike in poisoning incidents, particularly from:
- Increased use and accessibility of cleaning products
- Mixing of cleaning agents (especially bleach with acids, producing toxic chlorine gas)
- Products left in accessible locations during cleaning

## First Aid Protocol: Ingestion Poisoning in Children

### Immediate Steps

#### Step 1: Scene Safety and Initial Assessment
1. Ensure your own safety first
2. Check the child's consciousness, breathing, and airway
3. If the child is unconscious or not breathing, begin CPR and call 101 immediately

#### Step 2: Identify the Substance
1. Look for the container, packaging, or remnants of the substance
2. If substance is still in the child's mouth, attempt to remove it manually (wipe out with a cloth)
3. **Collect and preserve** the container, packaging, remaining substance, or any vomit for medical teams
4. Note the estimated amount ingested and the time of ingestion if possible

#### Step 3: Call for Help
- **If symptoms are present** (altered consciousness, breathing difficulty, burns in/around mouth, vomiting, abdominal pain): Call **101 (MDA)** immediately
- **If no symptoms are present**: Call the **Israel Poison Information Center at 04-7771900** for guidance on whether hospital evaluation is needed

#### Step 4: Do NOT Induce Vomiting
**This is the most critical rule.** Previous guidelines recommended inducing vomiting, but current Israeli medical protocol explicitly prohibits it because:
- The substance passing back through the oesophagus causes additional chemical burns (for caustic agents)
- Risk of aspiration (inhaling vomit into the lungs)
- Research shows uncontrolled vomiting removes only a partial amount of poison and is ineffective
- In one-third of cases where parents induced vomiting, it contradicted medical guidance

### Substance-Specific Protocols

#### Medications, Rodent Poison, and Similar Substances
- Do NOT induce vomiting
- Preserve the container/packaging
- Call Poison Center or 101

#### Acids (drain cleaners, toilet bowl cleaners) and Bases (oven cleaners, degreasers)
- **Absolutely do NOT induce vomiting** -- re-exposure causes additional oesophageal burns
- Do NOT attempt to induce vomiting using finger insertion, soapy water, or salt water
- **If the child is conscious and breathing normally**: give water or milk to dilute the substance
  - Children: approximately 1/4 to 1/2 cup (60-120 ml), depending on age
  - Adolescents/adults: one full cup
- Seek immediate medical assistance

#### Bleach (Economica)
- Do NOT induce vomiting
- If conscious and breathing: give diluting fluids as above
- Call Poison Center or 101

#### Limescale Removers
- If already diluted in kettle water, the concentration is significantly lower and poisoning risk is reduced (symptoms may be limited to sour taste and throat irritation)
- If undiluted: follow the acid/base dilution protocol above
- Seek medical evaluation

## First Aid Protocol: Skin or Eye Exposure

### Skin Contact
1. Remove contaminated clothing carefully
2. Flush the affected skin area with large amounts of running water for **15-20 minutes**
3. Do not apply creams, ointments, or other substances
4. Seek medical evaluation

### Eye Contact
1. Flush the eye(s) with large amounts of running water (room temperature) for at least **15-20 minutes**
2. Gently lift the eyelids to ensure thorough flushing
3. Do not rub the eyes
4. Seek immediate medical evaluation

## First Aid Protocol: Inhalation Poisoning

1. **Ensure your own safety** -- do not enter a confined space filled with toxic fumes without protection
2. Move the child to fresh air immediately
3. If the child is not breathing, begin CPR
4. Call 101 (MDA)
5. Contact fire department at 102 if hazardous gases or flammable materials are suspected

### Critical Warning: Never Mix Cleaning Products
Mixing bleach-based cleaners with other cleaning products (especially acids) releases toxic chlorine gas, which causes severe lung irritation and can trigger asthma attacks. This is a common cause of inhalation poisoning during household cleaning.

---

## Things You Must NOT Do

1. **Do NOT induce vomiting** -- regardless of the substance ingested
2. **Do NOT give activated charcoal** at home unless specifically instructed by the Poison Center or a physician
3. **Do NOT give food** to the poisoned child (water or milk for dilution purposes only, and only for caustic substances when the child is conscious)
4. **Do NOT attempt home remedies** (raw eggs, olive oil, etc.)
5. **Do NOT wait for symptoms** before calling for guidance -- some toxic substances cause no noticeable signs initially but cause severe organ damage (liver, kidneys) later
6. **Do NOT discard the container** -- always bring it to the hospital

## When to Call 101 (MDA) Immediately

- Child is unconscious or losing consciousness
- Difficulty breathing or respiratory distress
- Burns visible in or around the mouth
- Seizures or convulsions
- Severe vomiting
- Cyanosis (blue skin colouration)
- Known ingestion of a highly toxic substance (e.g., drain cleaner, medications in large quantity)

## Pediatric Considerations

- **"Children are not small adults"** (Rambam pediatric emergency guide) -- anatomical and physiological differences mean:
  - Smaller blood volumes (~800 ml in a 1-year-old vs. 5L in adults) -- toxins concentrate faster
  - Higher metabolic rate -- substances are absorbed more quickly
  - Medications safe for adults can be fatal for small children
  - Internal organs are less protected and more vulnerable
- Age-appropriate fluid amounts for dilution of caustic substances:
  - Toddlers: approximately 1/4 cup (60 ml)
  - Older children: approximately 1/2 cup (120 ml)
  - Adolescents: one full cup (240 ml)

## Prevention (Israeli Context)

### Storage
- Store all cleaning products, laundry chemicals, pesticides, and medications in high or locked cabinets
- Always return products to secure storage immediately after use
- Never transfer chemicals to food containers or unlabelled bottles
- Keep medications, cleaning agents, and pesticides separate from food

### Supervision
- Never leave young children unattended around cleaning or medication
- Avoid distractions while administering medication to children

### Specific Israeli Hazards
- **Laundry pods and dishwasher tablets**: Particularly dangerous for toddlers due to attractive, colourful appearance; burst easily and are toxic on contact with skin and mucous membranes
- **Pre-Passover cleaning**: Increased risk period due to heavy use of cleaning products
- **Pest control treatments**: Verify that applicators are registered with the Environmental Ministry; request documentation of materials used; obtain ventilation instructions

### Age-Specific Prevention
| Age Group | Key Prevention Measures |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Birth to 1 year | Locked/elevated storage of all chemicals and medications |
| 1-4 years | Same; never allow children to handle cleaning products |
| 5-14 years | Educate about alcohol and inhalant dangers; continue secure storage |
| 15-18 years | Explicit warnings about alcohol poisoning and substance abuse risks |
